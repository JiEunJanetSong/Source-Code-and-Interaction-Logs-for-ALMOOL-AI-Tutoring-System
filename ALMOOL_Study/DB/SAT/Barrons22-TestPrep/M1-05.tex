$$
4 x^n+x^{4 n}=
$$

\ansFOURs{%start
5 x 5 n}{%<--- (A)
x^n\left(4+x^{3 n}\right)}{%<--- (B)
5 x^{4 n 2}}{%<--- (C)
4 x^n\left(1+x^{3 n}\right)}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer: \textbf{(B)} 

Factor an $x^n$ out of the expression to get the answer:

$
4 x^n+x^{4 n}=x^n\left(4+x^{3 n}\right)
$


You cannot combine $x^n$ and $x^{4 n}$ into one term because they are not raised to the same power.

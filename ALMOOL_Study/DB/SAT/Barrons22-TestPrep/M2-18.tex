The final velocity of a given object is expressed by the following formula:

$$
\begin{gathered}
\text { Final velocity }= \\
\text { Initial velocity }+ \text { Acceleration } \times \text { Time }
\end{gathered}
$$

If a ball has an initial velocity of $4 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}$ and a constant acceleration of 6 $\mathrm{m} / \mathrm{s}^2$, which inequality shows the range of times, $T$, that will cause the final velocity to have a value of at least $22 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}$ ?

\ansFOURsT{%start
 $T \geq 3$ seconds}{%<--- (A)
 $T \geq 8$ seconds}{%<--- (B)
 $3$ seconds $\leq T \leq 5$ seconds}{%<--- (C)
 $6$ seconds $\leq T \leq 22$ seconds}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Solution}{%
Answer: \textbf{(A)} 

Plug the given values into the equation:

$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Final velocity} &= \text{Initial velocity} + \text{Acceleration} \times \text{Time} \\
\text{Initial velocity} &= 4 \, \mathrm{m/s} \\
\text{Acceleration} &= 6 \, \mathrm{m/s^2} \\
\text{Final velocity} &= 22 \, \mathrm{m/s}
\end{aligned}
$

The final velocity must be at least \(22 \, \mathrm{m/s}\). Set up the inequality so that the result for the final velocity could exceed \(22 \, \mathrm{m/s}\):

$
\begin{aligned}
22 &\leq 4 + 6T \\
18 &\leq 6T \\
3 &\leq T
\end{aligned}
$

}\SetValue{Answer}{%
A

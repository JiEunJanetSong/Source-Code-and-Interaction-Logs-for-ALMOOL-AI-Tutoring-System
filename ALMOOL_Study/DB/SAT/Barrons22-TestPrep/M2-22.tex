If $-16-6 x+x^2=x^2-a b x-8 b$, where $a$ and $b$ are constants, what is the value of $a$ ?

\ansFOURs{%start
 -6}{%<--- (A)
 -2}{%<--- (B)
 3}{%<--- (C)
 5}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Solution}{%
Answer: \textbf{(C)} 

We are given the equation:

$
-16 - 6x + x^2 = x^2 - abx - 8b
$

 Step 1: Eliminate $ x^2 $
Since $ x^2 $ appears on both sides, we can cancel it:

$
-16 - 6x = -abx - 8b
$

 Step 2: Compare Coefficients  
Rewriting the equation:

$
-6x - 16 = -abx - 8b
$

Comparing the coefficients of $ x $:

$
-6 = -ab
$

 Step 3: Solve for $ a $  
Dividing both sides by $ -b $:

$
a = \frac{6}{b}
$

From the constant term comparison:

$
-16 = -8b
$

$
b = 2
$

Substituting $ b = 2 $ into $ a = \frac{6}{b} $:

$
a = \frac{6}{2} = 3
$

 Step 4: Select the Correct Answer  
The correct choice is $ C) 3 $.

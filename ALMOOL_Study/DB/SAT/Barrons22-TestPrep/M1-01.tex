$$
\frac{m^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{m^{\tfrac{1}{2}}}=?
$$

\ansFOURs{%start
m}{%<--- (A)
m^2}{%<--- (B)

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer: \textbf{(B)}

Let's simplify the given expression:

$
\frac{m^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{m^{\tfrac{1}{2}}}
$

 Step 1: Apply Exponent Division Rule
Using the rule:

$
\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}
$

we subtract the exponents:

$
m^{\tfrac{5}{2} - \tfrac{1}{2}} = m^{\tfrac{4}{2}}
$

 Step 2: Simplify the Exponent
$
m^{\tfrac{4}{2}} = m^2
$

 Step 3: Select the Correct Answer
The correct choice is $B) m^2$.

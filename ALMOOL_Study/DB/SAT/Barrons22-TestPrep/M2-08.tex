Solve for $x$ in the equation below:

$$
4 x-y=\frac{1}{2}x-y+7
$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the equation:

$
4x - y = \frac{1}{2}x - y + 7
$

 Step 1: Eliminate $ y $
Since $ -y $ appears on both sides, we can cancel it:

$
4x = \frac{1}{2}x + 7
$

 Step 2: Move $ x $-Terms to One Side
Subtract $ \frac{1}{2}x $ from both sides:

$
4x - \frac{1}{2}x = 7
$

 Step 3: Simplify
$
\frac{8}{2}x - \frac{1}{2}x = 7
$

$
\frac{7}{2}x = 7
$

 Step 4: Solve for $ x $
Multiply both sides by $ \frac{2}{7} $:

$
x = \frac{7 \times 2}{7}
$

$
x = 2
$

 Step 5: Final Answer  
The solution is:

$
x = 2
$


}\SetValue{Answer}{%
2
}

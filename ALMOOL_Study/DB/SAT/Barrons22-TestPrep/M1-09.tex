How many real solution(s) does this system of equations have?

\begin{align*}
    y &= 2x - 5 \\
    y &= 2x^2 + 4
    \end{align*}
    
\ansFOURs{%start
 0}{%<--- (A)
 1}{%<--- (B)
 2}{%<--- (C)
 3}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer: \textbf{(A)}

Set the two equations equal to each other. Express the result as a quadratic equation:

$
2 x-5=2 x^2+4 \rightarrow 2 x^2-2 x+9=0
$

Plug these values for $a, b$, and $c$ into the quadratic equation. The only solutions are imaginary:

$
\dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4 a c}}{2 a}=\dfrac{2 \pm \sqrt{4-4 \times 18}}{2 \times 2}=\dfrac{2 \pm i \sqrt{68}}{4}
$

Therefore, there are zero solutions.

A pastry chef has a recipe that calls for 3 tablespoons of vanilla extract for a cake. The chef has misplaced his tablespoon and has only a teaspoon available-there are 3 teaspoons for each tablespoon. If the chef is making a total of two cakes, how many teaspoons of vanilla extract should he use?

\ansFOURs{%start
3}{%<--- (A)
6}{%<--- (B)
12}{%<--- (C)
18}%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Solution}{%
Answer: $\textbf{(D)}$ 

We are given that the recipe requires 3 tablespoons of vanilla extract for one cake. The chef is making two cakes, and there are 3 teaspoons in 1 tablespoon.

 Step 1: Calculate the Total Tablespoons for Two Cakes  

 $3 \text{ tbsp} \times 2 = 6 \text{ tbsp}$

 Step 2: Convert Tablespoons to Teaspoons  
$6 \text{ tbsp} \times 3 \dfrac{\text{tsp}} {\text{tbsp}} = 18 \text{ tsp}$

 Step 3: Select the Correct Answer  
The correct choice is $D) 18$.

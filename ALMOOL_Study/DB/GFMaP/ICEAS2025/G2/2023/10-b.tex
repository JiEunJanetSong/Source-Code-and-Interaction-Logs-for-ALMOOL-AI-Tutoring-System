In the diagram $P Q R S T$ is a circle with centre $O$. $P O R$ and $Q O T$ are straight lines. $\overline{Q T} \mid \overline{R S}, \angle R T Q=33^{\circ}$ and $|\overline{R S}|=|\overline{S T}|$. Find $\angle R S T$.

}\SetValue{Translation}{%

}\SetValue{Hint}{%



}\SetValue{GPTSolution}{%

}\SetValue{AltText}{%

The diagram depicts a circle with center $O$ and five points  on the circumference labeled as follows: starting from the bottom-left corner and moving anti-clockwise, the vertices are $P$, $Q$, $R$, $S$, and $T$. The points $P$, $O$, and $R$ are collinear, as well as the points $Q$, $O$, and $T$.  The following details are given:
- $QT \parallel RS$
- $\angle RTQ = 33^\circ$
- $RS = ST$ (There are also markings indicating that $RS$ and $ST$ are of equal length.)

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

(b) Since $\overline{QT} \parallel \overline{RS}$, $\angle RTQ = \angle TRS = 33^\circ$ (alternate angle), so $\angle TRS = 33^\circ$.

    Since $|\overline{RS}| = |\overline{ST}|$, from $\triangle RST$:

    $\angle TRS = \angle STR = 33^\circ$

    $\angle TRS + \angle STR + \angle RST = 180^\circ$

    $33^\circ + 33^\circ + \angle RST = 180^\circ$

    $66^\circ + \angle RST = 180^\circ$

    $\angle RST = 180^\circ - 66^\circ$

    $\angle RST = 114^\circ$

}\SetValue{Answer}{%

114

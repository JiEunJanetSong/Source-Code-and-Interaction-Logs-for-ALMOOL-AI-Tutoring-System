The volume of a cone with height $29\mathrm{~cm}$ is $1758 \mathrm{~cm}^3$. Find the radius of the cone. [Take $\pi=\frac{22}{7}$, Round to two decimal places.]

}\SetValue{Translation}{%

}\SetValue{Hint}{%

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given:

- The height of the cone $h = 29 \text{ cm}$.

- Its volume $V = 1758 \text{ cm}^3$. 

- We take $\pi = \tfrac{22}{7}$.  

The formula for the volume of a cone is
$$
V \;=\; \tfrac{1}{3}\,\pi\,r^2\,h.
$$

Plug in the known values:

$$
1758 
\;=\; \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times r^2 \times 29.
$$

Letâ€™s solve step by step.


1) Combine constants on the right

$$
\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 29 
=\; \frac{22 \times 29}{3 \times 7}
=\; \frac{638}{21}
=\; 30.38095\ldots
$$

So the equation becomes

$$
1758 
=\; (30.38095\ldots)\,r^2.
$$



2) Solve for $r^2$

$$
r^2 
=\; \frac{1758}{30.38095\ldots}
\;\approx\; 57.872.
$$



3) Solve for $r$

$$
r 
= \sqrt{57.872}
\;\approx\; 7.61 \text{ cm}.
$$

Rounding to two decimal places, the radius is

$$
\boxed{7.61\text{ cm}}.
$$

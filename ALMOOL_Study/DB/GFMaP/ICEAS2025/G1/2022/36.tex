The angle of a sector of a circle is $108^{\circ}$. If the radius of the circle is 3.5 cm, find the perimeter of the sector. [Take $\pi=\frac{22}{7}$, Express as an improper fraction.]

}\SetValue{Translation}{%

}\SetValue{Hint}{%



}\SetValue{GPTSolution}{%

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We have a sector of a circle with:

- Central angle $\theta = 108^\circ$.  

- Radius $r = 3.5 \text{ cm} = \tfrac{7}{2}\text{ cm}.$  

- $\pi = \frac{22}{7}.$

We want the perimeter of this sector. The perimeter is given by:

$$
\text{Perimeter} 
= \text{Arc length} + 2r.
$$

Step 1. Arc length

The length of the arc of a sector with angle $\theta$ (in degrees) in a circle of radius $r$ is:

$$
\text{Arc length} 
= \frac{\theta}{360^\circ} \times 2\pi r.
$$

Here,
$$
\frac{\theta}{360^\circ} 
= \frac{108}{360} 
= \frac{3}{10}.
$$

Thus,
$$
\text{Arc length} 
= \frac{3}{10} \times 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times \left(\frac{7}{2}\right).
$$

- Notice $3.5 = \tfrac{7}{2}$, so we use $\tfrac{7}{2}$ to keep everything as a fraction.
  
Letâ€™s simplify step by step:

1. $\frac{3}{10} \times 2 = \frac{6}{10} = \frac{3}{5}.$  

2. $\frac{3}{5} \times \frac{22}{7} = \frac{66}{35}.$  

3. $\frac{66}{35} \times \frac{7}{2} = \frac{66 \times 7}{35 \times 2} = \frac{462}{70} = \frac{66}{10} = \frac{33}{5}.$

Hence the arc length is
$$
\text{Arc length} = \frac{33}{5} \text{ cm}.
$$

Step 2. Add the two radii

The perimeter of the sector also includes the two straight sides (each of length $r$):

$$
2r = 2 \times 3.5 \text{ cm} = 7 \text{ cm} = \frac{35}{5}\text{ cm}.
$$

Step 3. Total perimeter

$$
\text{Perimeter} 
= \text{Arc length} + 2r 
= \frac{33}{5} + \frac{35}{5} 
= \frac{68}{5} \text{ cm}.
$$

So, expressed as an improper fraction (as requested), the perimeter of the sector is

$$
\boxed{\frac{68}{5} \text{ cm}}.
$$

An arc of a circle of radius $7 \mathrm{cm}$ is $14 \mathrm{cm}$ long. What angle does the arc subtend at the centre of the circle? [Take $\pi=\frac{22}{7}$, Round to two decimal places.]


}\SetValue{Translation}{%

}\SetValue{Hint}{%



}\SetValue{GPTSolution}{%

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We know the arc length $L$ of a circle of radius $r$ subtending an angle $\theta$ (in degrees) at the center is given by

$$
L \;=\; \frac{\theta}{360^\circ} \times 2 \pi r.
$$

Here:
$$
L = 14\text{ cm}, 
\quad r = 7\text{ cm}, 
\quad \pi = \tfrac{22}{7}.
$$

Plugging these into the formula:

$$
14 \;=\; \frac{\theta}{360} \times 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7.
$$

Step 1. Simplify the right side:

$$
2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 = 2 \times 22 = 44.
$$

So,

$$
14 \;=\; \frac{\theta}{360} \times 44 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad 
14 \times 360 \;=\; 44\,\theta \quad\Longrightarrow\quad 
5040 = 44\,\theta \quad\Longrightarrow\quad 
\theta = \frac{5040}{44} \approx 114.5454^\circ.
$$

Step 2. Round to two decimal places:

$$
\theta \approx 114.55^\circ.
$$

Therefore, the angle subtended at the center is $\boxed{114.55^\circ}$ (to two decimal places).

}\SetValue{Answer}{%

114.55

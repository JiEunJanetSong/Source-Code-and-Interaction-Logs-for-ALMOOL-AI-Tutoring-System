A rectangular tank is $80\mathrm{cm}$ long, $60\mathrm{cm}$ wide and $50\mathrm{cm}$ high. How many litres of water can the tank hold?

}\SetValue{Translation}{%

}\SetValue{Hint}{%



}\SetValue{GPTSolution}{%

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find how many liters of water the rectangular tank can hold:

1. Calculate the volume in cubic centimeters:

$$
\text{Volume} = \text{length} \times \text{width} \times \text{height} 
= 80 \text{ cm} \times 60 \text{ cm} \times 50 \text{ cm} 
= 240{,}000 \text{ cm}^3.
$$

2. Convert cubic centimeters to liters:

Recall that $1 \text{ liter} = 1000 \text{ cm}^3$. Therefore,

$$
\text{Volume in liters} = \frac{240{,}000 \text{ cm}^3}{1000 \text{ cm}^3/\text{liter}} = 240 \text{ liters}.
$$

Hence, the tank can hold $\boxed{240 \text{ liters}}$ of water.

}\SetValue{Answer}{%

240

Given that $\sin p=\dfrac{5}{13}$, where $p$ is acute. find the value of $(\cos p-\tan p)$.

}\SetValue{Translation}{%

}\SetValue{Hint}{%



}\SetValue{GPTSolution}{%

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

First, note that $\sin p = \frac{5}{13}$ and $p$ is acute (so $0 < p < \frac{\pi}{2}$). 

1. Compute $\cos p$:  
$$
\cos p = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 p} 
= \sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{5}{13}\right)^2} 
= \sqrt{1 - \frac{25}{169}} 
= \sqrt{\frac{169 - 25}{169}} 
= \sqrt{\frac{144}{169}} 
= \frac{12}{13}.
$$

2. Compute $\tan p$:  
$$
\tan p = \frac{\sin p}{\cos p} 
= \frac{\frac{5}{13}}{\frac{12}{13}} 
= \frac{5}{12}.
$$

3. Evaluate $\cos p - \tan p$:
$$
\cos p - \tan p 
= \frac{12}{13} - \frac{5}{12} 
= \frac{144}{156} - \frac{65}{156} 
= \frac{79}{156}.
$$

Hence,
$$
\cos p - \tan p = \frac{79}{156}.
$$

}\SetValue{Answer}{%

79/156

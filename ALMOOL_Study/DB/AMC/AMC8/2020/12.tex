For a positive integer $n$, the factorial notation $n$ ! represents the product of the integers from $n$ to 1 . (For example, $6!=6 \cdot 5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1$.) What value of $N$ satisfies the following equation?

$$
5!\cdot 9!=12 \cdot N!
$$

	\ansFIVEs{%start
	 10}{%<--- (A)
	 11}{%<--- (B)
	 12}{%<--- (C)
	 13}{%<---- (D)
	 14}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (A): The given equation implies that

$$
N!=\frac{5!\cdot 9!}{12}=\frac{(5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1) \cdot(9!)}{12}=10 \cdot 9!=10!
$$

so $N=10$.

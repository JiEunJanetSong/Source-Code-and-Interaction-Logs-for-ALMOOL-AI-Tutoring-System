Rectangles $R_1$ and $R_2$, and squares $S_1, S_2$, and $S_3$, shown below, combine to form a rectangle that is 3322 units wide and 2020 units high. What is the side length of $S_2$ in units?

\begin{center}		
    \includegraphics[scale=0.6]{AMC-8-pics/2020-25.png}
	\end{center}
	
\ansFIVEs{%start
		651 }{%<--- (A)
		655 }{%<--- (B)
		656 }{%<--- (C)
		662 }{%<---- (D)
		666 }%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (A): Let $x_1, x_2$, and $x_3$ represent the side lengths of squares $S_1, S_2$, and $S_3$, respectively. Then the width of the large rectangle equals

$$
x_1+x_2+x_3=3322
$$

and the height of the large rectangle equals

$$
x_1-x_2+x_3=2020
$$


Subtracting the two equations yields $2 x_2=1302$, so $x_2=651$. The side length of $S_2$ is therefore 651 units.

Note: Although the size of square $S_2$ is fixed, the sizes of $S_1$ and $S_3$ can vary as long as the sum of their side lengths is $x_1+x_3=3322-x_2=2671$.

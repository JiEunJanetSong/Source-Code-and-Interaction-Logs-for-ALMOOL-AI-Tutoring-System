Suppose $15 \%$ of $x$ equals $20 \%$ of $y$. What percentage of $x$ is $y$?

	\ansFIVEs{%start
	5}{%<--- (A)
	35}{%<--- (B)
	75}{%<--- (C)
	133 \frac{1}{3}}{%<---- (D)
	300}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (C): First note that $15 \%=0.15$, and $20 \%=0.20$. It is given that $15 \%$ of $x$ equals $20 \%$ of $y$, so

$$
0.15 x=0.20 y
$$


Solving for $y$ yields

$$
y=\frac{0.15}{0.20} x=\frac{15}{20} x=\frac{75}{100} x
$$


Therefore $y$ is $75 \%$ of $x$.

OR

Let $x=100$. Then $15 \%$ of $x$ is 15 . This equals $20 \%$ of $y$, which is the same as $\frac{1}{5} y$. Solving the equation $15=\frac{1}{5} y$ yields $y=5 \cdot 15=75$. Therefore $y$ is $75 \%$ of $x$.

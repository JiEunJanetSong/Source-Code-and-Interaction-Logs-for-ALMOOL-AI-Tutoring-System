When the World Wide Web first became popular in the 1990s, download speeds reached a maximum of about 56 kilobits per second. Approximately how many minutes would the download of a 4.2-megabyte song have taken at that speed? (Note that there are 8000 kilobits in a megabyte.)

\ansFIVEs{%start
		 0.6 }{%<--- (A)
		 10 }{%<--- (B)
		 1800 }{%<--- (C)
		 7200 }{%<---- (D)
		 36000 }%<---- (D)
		 
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given:

- Download speed: 56 kilobits per second

- File size: 4.2 megabytes

- Conversion: 1 megabyte $=8000$ kilobits

Step 1: Convert megabytes to kilobits

$$
4.2 \mathrm{MB}=4.2 \times 8000=33,600 \text { kilobits }
$$


Step 2: Time (in seconds) to download

$$
\text { Time }=\frac{33,600 \text { kilobits }}{56 \text { kbps }}=600 \text { seconds }
$$


Step 3: Convert seconds to minutes

$$
\frac{600}{60}=10 \text { minutes }
$$


Answer: (B) 10

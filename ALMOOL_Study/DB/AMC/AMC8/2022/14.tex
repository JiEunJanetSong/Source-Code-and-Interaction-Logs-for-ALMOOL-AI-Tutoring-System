In how many ways can the letters in BEEKEEPER be rearranged so that two or more Es do not appear together?

\ansFIVEs{%start
		 1 }{%<--- (A)
		 4 }{%<--- (B)
		 12 }{%<--- (C)
		 24 }{%<---- (D)
		 120 }%<---- (D)
		 
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the word BEEKEEPER and asked:

In how many ways can the letters be rearranged so that no two or more Es appear together?

Step 1: Count the Es

- BEEKEEPER has 5 Es

- Other letters: B, K, P, R (4 unique non-E letters)

Step 2: Place Es so that no two are adjacent

We must place Es in separate slots, with at least one non-E letter between any two Es.

So, we first place the 4 non-E letters (B, K, P, R) in a line:

$$
\underline{\quad} B \underline{\quad} K \underline{\quad} P \underline{\quad} R \underline{\quad}
$$

This gives 5 slots between/around the letters where Es can be placed, and no two Es will be adjacent if we place them in separate slots.

Step 3: Choose 5 slots from 5

We must place the 5 Es in these 5 slots - one E per slot, so only 1 way to do it.

But we can permute the 4 non-E letters in $4!=24$ ways.

Final Answer: (D) 24

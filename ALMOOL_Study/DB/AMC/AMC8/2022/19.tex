Mr. Ramos gave a test to his class of 20 students. The dot plot below shows the distribution of test scores.

\begin{center}

	Later Mr. Ramos discovered that there was a scoring error on one of the questions. He regraded the tests, awarding some of the students 5 extra points, which increased the median test score to 85. What is the minimum number of students who received extra points?
	(Note that the median test score equals the average of the 2 scores in the middle if the 20 test scores are arranged in increasing order.)

\ansFIVEs{%start
		2 }{%<--- (A)
		3 }{%<--- (B)
		4 }{%<--- (C)
		5 }{%<---- (D)
		6 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%

라모스 선생님은 20명의 학생으로 구성된 반에서 시험을 치렀습니다. 아래 점선은 시험 점수의 분포를 보여줍니다.

나중에 라모스 씨는 문제 중 하나에 채점 오류가 있음을 발견했습니다. 그는 시험을 재채점하여 일부 학생에게 5점의 가산점을 부여하여 시험 평균 점수를 85점으로 올렸습니다. 추가 점수를 받은 학생의 최소 인원은 몇 명인가요? (시험 점수 중앙값은 20개의 시험 점수를 높은 순서로 배열한 경우 가운데에 있는 2개의 점수의 평균과 같습니다.)
}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Given:

- 20 students $\rightarrow$ median $=$ average of the 10 th and 11 th scores

- Original median:

$$
\frac{75+75}{2}=75
$$

- New median $=85$

Step 1: Count how many students scored below 85

From the dot plot:

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|}
\hline Score & Count & Cumulative \\
\hline 60 & 2 & 2 \\
\hline 65 & 4 & 6 \\
\hline 70 & 2 & 8 \\
\hline 75 & 4 & $12 \leftarrow$ includes 10th \& 11th scores \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

So, the 10th and 11th students originally scored 75.

Step 2: Raise scores to make 10th and 11th $\geq 85$

To do this, we must push at least 4 students who scored below 85 up to 85 or higher, so they move above the 10th/11th position.

If 4 students who had 75 get +5 points $(75 \rightarrow 80)$, they are still not $\geq 85$. But if they get $+10(75 \rightarrow 85)$, now:

- Number of students scoring 85 increases from 2 to 6

- 85 becomes the new 10th and 11th scores

Final Answer: (C) 4

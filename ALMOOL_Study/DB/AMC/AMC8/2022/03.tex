When three positive integers $a, b$, and $c$ are multiplied together, their product is 100. Suppose $a<b<c$. In how many ways can the numbers be chosen?

\ansFIVEs{%start
		0 }{%<--- (A)
		1 }{%<--- (B)
		2 }{%<--- (C)
		3 }{%<---- (D)
		4 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given that $a b c=100$ and $a<b<c$, where $a, b, c$ are positive integers.

Step 1: List all divisors of 100

$$
1,2,4,5,10,20,25,50,100
$$


Step 2: Find valid triples $(a, b, c)$ such that $a b c=100$ and $a<$ $b<c$

Valid combinations:

- $(1,2,50)$

- $(1,4,25)$

- $(1,5,20)$

- $(2,5,10)$

Final Answer: 4

Answer: (E) 4

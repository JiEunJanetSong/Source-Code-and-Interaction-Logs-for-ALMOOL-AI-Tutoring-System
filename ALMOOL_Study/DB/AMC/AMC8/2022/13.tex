How many positive integers can fill the blank in the sentence below?

``One positive integer is $\underline{\qquad}$ more than twice another, and the sum of the two numbers is 28.''

\ansFIVEs{%start
		 6 }{%<--- (A)
		 7 }{%<--- (B)
		 8 }{%<--- (C)
		 9 }{%<---- (D)
		 10 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%

아래 문장에서 빈칸을 채울 수 있는 양의 정수는 몇 개입니까? 

'하나의 양의 정수는 $\밑줄$가 다른 양의 두 배 이상이고, 두 수의 합은 28입니다.'''

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Let's define:

- Smaller number $=n$

- Larger number $=m=2 n+d$, where $d$ is the number that fills the blank

Also, we're told:

$$
m+n=28
$$


Step 1: Substitute $m=2 n+d$

$$
(2 n+d)+n=28 \Rightarrow 3 n+d=28 \Rightarrow d=28-3 n
$$


Step 2: Count how many positive integers $d$ can be

We need $d>0 \Rightarrow 28-3 n>0 \Rightarrow n<\frac{28}{3} \approx 9.33$

Since $n$ must be a positive integer, $n=1,2, \ldots, 9 \rightarrow 9$ values

Final Answer: (D) 9

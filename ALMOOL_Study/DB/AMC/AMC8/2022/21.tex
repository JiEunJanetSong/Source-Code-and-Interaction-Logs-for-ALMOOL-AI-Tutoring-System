Steph scored 15 baskets out of 20 attempts in the first half of a game, and 10 baskets out of 10 attempts in the second half. Candace took 12 attempts in the first half and 18 attempts in the second. In each half, Steph scored a higher percentage of baskets than Candace.

Surprisingly they ended with the same overall percentage of baskets scored. How many more baskets did Candace score in the second half than in the first?

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Steph's performance

- First half: $\frac{15}{20}=75 \%$

- Second half: $\frac{10}{10}=100 \%$

- Total:

$$
\begin{gathered}
\text { Total made }=15+10=25, \quad \text { Total attempts }=20+10=30 \\
\text { Overall percentage }=\frac{25}{30}=\frac{5}{6} \approx 83.33 \%
\end{gathered}
$$


Step 2: Let Candace's successful baskets be:

- First half: $x$ out of 12

- Second half: $y$ out of 18

Total:

- Attempts $=12+18=30$

- Made $=x+y$

We are told:

$$
\frac{x+y}{30}=\frac{25}{30} \Rightarrow x+y=25
$$


Also:

- $\frac{x}{12}<\frac{15}{20}=0.75 \Rightarrow x<9$

- $\frac{y}{18}<\frac{10}{10}=1.00 \Rightarrow y<18$

Step 3: Solve under constraints

From (1): $x+y=25$

Try values of $x<9$ :

- If $x=8$, then $y=17$

- Check:

- $\frac{8}{12}=0. \overline{66}<0.75$

- $\frac{17}{18} \approx 0.944<1$

$\rightarrow$ Valid solution

Final Step: Difference in baskets between halves

$$
y-x=17-8=9
$$

(C) 9

A cup of boiling water $\left(212^{\circ} \mathrm{F}\right)$ is placed to cool in a room whose temperature remains constant at $68^{\circ} \mathrm{F}$. Suppose the difference between the water temperature and the room temperature is halved every 5 minutes. What is the water temperature, in degrees Fahrenheit, after 15 minutes?

\ansFIVEs{%start
		 77 }{%<--- (A)
		 86 }{%<--- (B)
		 92 }{%<--- (C)
		 98 }{%<---- (D)
		 104 }%<---- (D)
		 
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are told:

- Initial temperature: $212^{\circ} \mathrm{F}$

- Room temperature: $68^{\circ} \mathrm{F}$

- Every 5 minutes, the temperature difference is halved

- Find water temperature after 15 minutes

Step 1: Initial difference

$$
212-68=144^{\circ}
$$


Step 2: Halve the difference every 5 minutes

- After 5 min: $144 \div 2=72$

- After $10 \mathrm{~min}: 72 \div 2=36$

- After 15 min: $36 \div 2=18$

Step 3: Add back to room temp

$$
68+18=86^{\circ} \mathrm{F}
$$


Answer: (B) 86

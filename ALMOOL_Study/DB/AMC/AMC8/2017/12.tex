The smallest positive integer greater than 1 that leaves a remainder of 1 when divided by 4, 5, and 6 lies between which of the following pairs of numbers?

\ansFIVEsT{%start
		$2$ and $19$ }{%<--- (A)
		$20$ and $39$ }{%<--- (B)
		$40$ and $59$ }{%<--- (C)
		$60$ and $79$ }{%<---- (D)
		$80$ and $124$ }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (D): The least common multiple of 4, 5, and 6 is 60. Numbers that leave a remainder of 1 when divided by 4, 5, and 6 are 1 more than a whole number multiple of 60. So the smallest positive number greater than 1 that leaves a remainder of 1 when divided by 4, 5, and 6 is 61.

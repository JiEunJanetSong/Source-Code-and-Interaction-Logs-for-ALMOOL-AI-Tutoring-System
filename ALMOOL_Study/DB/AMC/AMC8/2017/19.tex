For any positive integer $M$, the notation $M$! denotes the product of the integers 1 through $M$. What is the largest integer $n$ for which $5^n$ is a factor of the sum $98!+99!+100!?$

\ansFIVEs{%start
		23 }{%<--- (A)
		24 }{%<--- (B)
		25 }{%<--- (C)
		26 }{%<---- (D)
		27 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (D): Factoring yields $98!+99!+100!=98!(1+99+100 \cdot 99)=98!(100+100 \cdot 99)=$ $98!(100)(1+99)=98!\cdot 100^2$. The exponent of 5 in $98!$ is $19+3=22$, one for each multiple of 5 and one more for each multiple of 25 . Thus the exponent of 5 in the product is $22+4=26$ as $100^2=2^4 \cdot 5^4$.

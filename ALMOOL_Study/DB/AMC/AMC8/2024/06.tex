Sergai skated around an ice rink, gliding along different paths. The gray lines in the figures below show four of the paths labeled $\mathrm{P}, \mathrm{Q}, \mathrm{R}$, and S. What is the sorted order of the four paths from shortest to longest?

\begin{center}		
    \includegraphics[scale=0.6]{AMC-8-pics/2024-06.png}
	\end{center}

\ansFIVEs{%start
		P, Q, R, S }{%<--- (A)
		P, R, S, Q }{%<--- (B)
		Q, S, P, R }{%<--- (C)
		R, P, S, Q }{%<---- (D)
		R, S, P, Q }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

You can measure the lengths of the paths until you find a couple of guaranteed true inferred statements as such: $Q$ is greater than $S, P$ is greater than $R$, and $R$ and $P$ are the smallest two, therefore the order is $R, P, S, Q$. Thus we get the answer
(D) $R, P, S, Q$

In triangle $A B C$, point $D$ divides side $\overline{A C}$ so that $A D: D C=1: 2$. Let $E$ be the midpoint of $\overline{B D}$ and let $F$ be the point of intersection of line $B C$ and line $A E$. Given that the area of $\triangle A B C$ is 360, what is the area of $\triangle E B F ?$

	\end{center}

	Let $G$ be the point on side $B C$ such that $\overline{D G}$ is parallel to $\overline{A F}$. Then $B F=F G$ because $\triangle B E F$ and $\triangle B D G$ are similar and $B E=E D$. Also $2 F G=G C$ because $\triangle A C F$ and $\triangle D C G$ are similar and $2 A D=D C$. Thus $B C=B F+F G+G C=4 B F$. Let $[X Y Z]$ denote the area of a triangle $X Y Z$. Then

$$
[B D C]=\frac{2}{3}[B A C]=240
$$

because $D C=2 A D$ and the triangles have the same altitude to line $A C$. Draw segment $\overline{F D}$. Similarly, $[B D F]=\frac{1}{4}[B D C]=60$. Therefore $[B E F]=\frac{1}{2}[B D F]=30$.

OR

Because triangles $A B D$ and $D B C$ share the same altitude to line $A C$ and $A D: D C=1: 2$, the ratio of the areas $[A B D]:[D B C]$ also is $1: 2$. Draw segment $\overline{F D}$. Similarly the ratio $[A F D]:[D F C]$ is $1: 2$.
Let $x$ represent the area of $\triangle E B F$. Then because $B E=E D$, the areas $[A B E]$ and $[A E D]$ are equal and $[E B F]=[D E F]=x$.
Given that the area of $\triangle A B C$ is 360 , we can calculate the following areas:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& {[A B D]=\frac{1}{3}[A B C]=\frac{1}{3}(360)=120} \\
& {[D B C]=\frac{2}{3}[A B C]=\frac{2}{3}(360)=240} \\
& {[A B E]=[A E D]=\frac{1}{2}[A B D]=\frac{1}{2}(120)=60}
\end{aligned}
$$

Then using the ratio of the areas of $\triangle A F D$ and $\triangle D F C$ we can solve for $x$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{[A F D]}{[D F C]} & =\frac{1}{2} \\
\frac{[A E D]+[D E F]}{[D B C]-[D B F]} & =\frac{1}{2} \\
\frac{60+x}{240-2 x} & =\frac{1}{2} \\
120+2 x & =240-2 x \\
4 x & =120 \\
x & =30
\end{aligned}
$$


Therefore the area of $\triangle E B F$ is 30.

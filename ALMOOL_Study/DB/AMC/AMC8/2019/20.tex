How many different real numbers $x$ satisfy the equation

$$
\left(x^2-5\right)^2=16?
$$

	\ansFIVEs{%start
		0}{%<--- (A)
		1 }{%<--- (B)
		2 }{%<--- (C)
		4 }{%<---- (D)
	8}%<---- (D)

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

If $x$ is a real number satisfying $\left(x^2-5\right)^2=16$ then either $x^2-5=4$ or $x^2-5=-4$. There are two numbers, 3 and -3, that satisfy the first equation and two additional numbers, 1 and -1, that satisfy the second equation. Thus there are four distinct solutions in all.

The twelve-sided figure shown has been drawn on $1 \mathrm{~cm} \times 1 \mathrm{~cm}$ graph paper. What is the area of the figure in $\mathrm{cm}^2$?

\begin{center}		
    \includegraphics[scale=0.6]{AMC-8-pics/2018-04.png}
	\end{center}

\ansFIVEs{%start
		12 }{%<--- (A)
		12.5 }{%<--- (B)
		13 }{%<--- (C)
		13.5 }{%<---- (D)
		14 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (C):
In the figure below, the square $R S T U$ has area $3 \cdot 3=9$, and $\triangle R E F$ has area $\frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 \cdot 1=1$. The other three triangles are congruent to $\triangle R E F$, so the total area is $9+4 \cdot 1=13$.

How many perfect cubes lie between $2^8+1$ and $2^{18}+1$, inclusive?

\ansFIVEs{%start
		4 }{%<--- (A)
		9 }{%<--- (B)
		10 }{%<--- (C)
		57 }{%<---- (D)
		58 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (E): Note that $2^8+1=257$ and that $216=6^3<257<7^3=343$. Also note that $2^{18}=\left(2^6\right)^3=64^3$, so the perfect cube that is closest to and less than $2^{18}+1$ is $64^3$. Thus the numbers $7^3, 8^3, \ldots, 63^3, 64^3$ are precisely the perfect cubes that lie between the two given numbers, and so there are $64-6=58$ of these perfect cubes.

The harmonic mean of a set of non-zero numbers is the reciprocal of the average of the reciprocals of the numbers. What is the harmonic mean of 1, 2, and 4?

\ansFIVEs{%start
		\frac{3}{7} }{%<--- (A)
		\frac{7}{12} }{%<--- (B)
		\frac{12}{7} }{%<--- (C)
		\frac{7}{4} }{%<---- (D)
		\frac{7}{3} }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (C): The reciprocals of 1, 2, and 4 are 1, $\frac{1}{2}$, and $\frac{1}{4}$, and their average is $\frac{1+\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{4}}{3}=\frac{7}{12}$. So the harmonic mean of 1,2 , and 4 is the reciprocal of $\frac{7}{12}$, which is $\frac{12}{7}$.

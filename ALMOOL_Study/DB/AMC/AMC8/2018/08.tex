Mr. Garcia asked the members of his health class how many days last week they exercised for at least 30 minutes. The results are summarized in the following bar graph, where the heights of the bars represent the number of students.

\begin{center}		
    \includegraphics[scale=0.6]{AMC-8-pics/2018-08.png}
	\end{center}

	What was the mean number of days of exercise last week, rounded to the nearest hundredth, reported by the students in Mr. Garcia's class?

\ansFIVEs{%start
		3.50 }{%<--- (A)
		3.57 }{%<--- (B)
		4.36 }{%<--- (C)
		4.50 }{%<---- (D)
		5.00 }%<---- (D)
}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Answer (C): The number of students surveyed was $1+3+2+6+8+3+2=25$, and the number of days of exercise reported was $1 \cdot 1+3 \cdot 2+2 \cdot 3+6 \cdot 4+8 \cdot 5+3 \cdot 6+2 \cdot 7=109$. So the mean number of days of exercise was $109 \div 25=4.36$.

Find the differential equation of the form $y^{\prime}=f(y)$ satisfied by the function

$$
y(t)=8 e^{5 t}-\frac{2}{5}.
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given a function:

$$
y(t)=8 e^{5 t}-\frac{2}{5}
$$


We are asked to find a differential equation of the form $y^{\prime}=f(y)$, that is, an equation that expresses $y^{\prime}$ in terms of $y$ alone.

Step 1: Compute $y^{\prime}$

Differentiate $y(t)$ with respect to $t$ :

$$
y^{\prime}(t)=\frac{d}{d t}\left(8 e^{5 t}-\frac{2}{5}\right)=8 \cdot 5 e^{5 t}=40 e^{5 t}
$$


Step 2: Express $e^{5 t}$ in terms of $y$

From the original expression:

$$
y=8 e^{5 t}-\frac{2}{5} \Rightarrow y+\frac{2}{5}=8 e^{5 t} \Rightarrow e^{5 t}=\frac{y+\frac{2}{5}}{8}
$$


Step 3: Substitute into $y^{\prime}$

Now, plug the expression for $e^{5 t}$ into the derivative:

$$
y^{\prime}=40 e^{5 t}=40 \cdot \frac{y+\frac{2}{5}}{8}=5\left(y+\frac{2}{5}\right)
$$


Simplify:

$$
y^{\prime}=5 y+2
$$


Final Answer:

$$
y^{\prime}=5 y+2
$$

Find all solutions of

$$
y^{\prime}=2 y+5
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the first-order linear differential equation:

$$
y^{\prime}=2 y+5
$$


Step 1: Write in standard form

$$
y^{\prime}-2 y=5
$$


This is a linear differential equation of the form:

$$
y^{\prime}+P(t) y=Q(t)
$$

with:
- $P(t)=-2$
- $Q(t)=5$

Step 2: Find the integrating factor

$$
\mu(t)=e^{\int-2 d t}=e^{-2 t}
$$


Step 3: Multiply both sides by the integrating factor

$$
e^{-2 t}\left(y^{\prime}-2 y\right)=5 e^{-2 t} \Rightarrow \frac{d}{d t}\left(e^{-2 t} y\right)=5 e^{-2 t}
$$


Step 4: Integrate both sides

$$
\int \frac{d}{d t}\left(e^{-2 t} y\right) d t=\int 5 e^{-2 t} d t
$$


Left-hand side:

$$
e^{-2 t} y
$$


Right-hand side:

$$
\int 5 e^{-2 t} d t=5 \cdot \frac{e^{-2 t}}{-2}=-\frac{5}{2} e^{-2 t}+C
$$


So:

$$
e^{-2 t} y=-\frac{5}{2} e^{-2 t}+C
$$

Step 5: Solve for $y(t)$

Multiply both sides by $e^{2 t}$:

$$
y(t)=-\frac{5}{2}+C e^{2 t}
$$


Final Answer:

$y(t)=C e^{2 t}-\frac{5}{2}, \quad$ for all $C \in \mathbb{R}$

Follow the steps below to solve

$$
y^{\prime}=-3 y+5, \quad y(0)=1
$$

(a) Find any integrating factor $\mu$ for the differential equation.

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the initial value problem:

$$
y^{\prime}=-3 y+5, \quad y(0)=1
$$


Step (a): Write the equation in standard linear form

We rewrite the equation as:

$$
y^{\prime}+3 y=5
$$


This is a linear first-order ODE of the form:

$$
y^{\prime}+P(t) y=Q(t)
$$

with:

- $P(t)=3$

- $Q(t)=5$

Step (a): Find an integrating factor $\mu(t)$

The integrating factor is given by:

$$
\mu(t)=e^{\int P(t) d t}=e^{\int 3 d t}=e^{3 t}
$$


Final Answer for (a):

$$
\mu(t)=e^{3 t}
$$

Find the solution of the IVP

$$
y^{\prime}=6 y+1, \quad y(0)=1
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the initial value problem (IVP):

$$
y^{\prime}=6 y+1, \quad y(0)=1
$$


Step 1: Solve the differential equation

We already found the general solution in the previous step:

$$
y(t)=C e^{6 t}-\frac{1}{6}
$$


Step 2: Apply the initial condition $y(0)=1$

Substitute $t=0$ :

$$
y(0)=C e^0-\frac{1}{6}=C-\frac{1}{6} \Rightarrow 1=C-\frac{1}{6} \Rightarrow C=1+\frac{1}{6}=\frac{7}{6}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
y(t)=\frac{7}{6} e^{6 t}-\frac{1}{6}
$$

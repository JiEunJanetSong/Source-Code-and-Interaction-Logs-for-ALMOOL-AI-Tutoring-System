Follow the steps below to find all solutions of

$$
y^{\prime}=-4 y+2
$$

(d) Compute $y$ using part (c).

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

From part (c), we integrated the equation $\psi^{\prime}(t)=0$, where $\psi(t, y)=e^{4 t} y-\frac{1}{2} e^{4 t}$. The result of this integration was:

$$
e^{4 t} y-\frac{1}{2} e^{4 t}=C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.
To compute $y$, we need to solve this equation for $y(t)$ :
$\$ e^{4 t} y=C+\frac{1}{2} e^{4 t} \$$ Divide both sides by $e^{4 t}$ :

$$
\begin{aligned}
& y(t)=\frac{C+\frac{1}{2} e^{4 t}}{e^{4 t}} \\
& y(t)=\frac{C}{e^{4 t}}+\frac{\frac{1}{2} e^{4 t}}{e^{4 t}} \\
& y(t)=C e^{-4 t}+\frac{1}{2}
\end{aligned}
$$


This expression for $y(t)$ represents all solutions to the differential equation $y^{\prime}=-4 y+2$. The different solutions are obtained by choosing different values for the constant $C$.

The final answer is $y(t)=C e^{-4 t}+\frac{1}{2} , \quad$ for all $C \in \mathbb{R}$

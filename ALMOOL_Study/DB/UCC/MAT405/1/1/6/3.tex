Find all solutions $y$ of

$$
y^{\prime}=3 y
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the first-order linear differential equation:

$$
y^{\prime}=3 y
$$


This is a separable differential equation. Let's solve it step by step.

Step 1: Separate the variables

$$
\frac{d y}{d t}=3 y \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{y} d y=3 d t \quad(\text { assuming } y \neq 0)
$$


Step 2: Integrate both sides

$$
\int \frac{1}{y} d y=\int 3 d t \Rightarrow \ln |y|=3 t+C
$$


Step 3: Solve for $y$

Exponentiate both sides:

$$
|y|=e^{3 t+C}=e^C \cdot e^{3 t} \Rightarrow y= \pm e^C \cdot e^{3 t}
$$


Let $A= \pm e^C \in \mathbb{R}$. Then the general solution is:

$$
y(t)=A e^{3 t}, \quad \text { for any constant } A \in \mathbb{R}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
y(t)=A e^{3 t} \quad \text { for all } A \in \mathbb{R}
$$

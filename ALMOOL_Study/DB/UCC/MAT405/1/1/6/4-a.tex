Follow the steps below to find all solutions of

$$
y^{\prime}=-4 y+2
$$

(a) Find the integrating factor $\mu$.

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the first-order linear differential equation:

$$
y^{\prime}=-4 y+2
$$


Let's follow the steps to solve it. The standard form for applying an integrating factor is:

$$
y^{\prime}+P(t) y=Q(t)
$$


Step (a): Write in standard form

$$
y^{\prime}+4 y=2
$$


Now we identify:

- $P(t)=4$

- $Q(t)=2$

Step (a): Find the integrating factor $\mu(t)$

The integrating factor $\mu(t)$ is defined as:

$$
\mu(t)=e^{\int P(t) d t}=e^{\int 4 d t}=e^{4 t}
$$


Final Answer for (a):

$$
\mu(t)=e^{4 t}
$$

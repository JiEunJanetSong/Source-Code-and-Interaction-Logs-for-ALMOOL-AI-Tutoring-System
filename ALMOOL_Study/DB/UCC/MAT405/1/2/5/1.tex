Find all solutions of

$$
y^{\prime}=4 t y
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the first-order differential equation:

$$
y^{\prime}=4 t y
$$


This can be written in the standard linear form:

$$
y^{\prime}-4 t y=0
$$

which is a first-order linear differential equation with a variable coefficient. To solve it, we apply the integrating factor method.

Step 1: Identify the coefficient function

We rewrite the equation in the form:

$$
y^{\prime}+p(t) y=g(t)
$$


In our case:

- $p(t)=-4 t$

- $g(t)=0$

Step 2: Compute the integrating factor

The integrating factor $\mu(t)$ is defined as:

$$
\mu(t)=e^{\int p(t) d t}=e^{\int-4 t d t}=e^{-2 t^2}
$$


Step 3: Multiply through by the integrating factor

Multiply both sides of the differential equation by $\mu(t)$ :

$$
e^{-2 t^2} y^{\prime}-4 t e^{-2 t^2} y=0
$$


The left-hand side is now the derivative of the product:

$$
\frac{d}{d t}\left(e^{-2 t^2} y\right)=0
$$

Step 4: Integrate both sides

$$
\frac{d}{d t}\left(e^{-2 t^2} y\right)=0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad e^{-2 t^2} y=C
$$

where $C \in \mathbb{R}$ is the constant of integration.

Step 5: Solve for $y(t)$

$$
y(t)=C e^{2 t^2}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
y(t)=C e^{2 t^2}, \quad C \in \mathbb{R}
$$

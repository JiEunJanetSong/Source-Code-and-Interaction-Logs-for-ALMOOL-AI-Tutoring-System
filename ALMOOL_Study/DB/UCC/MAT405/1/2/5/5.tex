Find all solutions $y$ to the ODE

$$
\frac{y^{\prime}}{\left(t^2+1\right) y}=4 t
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Multiply both sides to isolate $y^{\prime}$
Multiply both sides by $\left(t^2+1\right) y$ :

$$
y^{\prime}=4 t\left(t^2+1\right) y
$$


This is a first-order separable ODE.

Step 2: Separate the variables

$$
\frac{d y}{y}=4 t\left(t^2+1\right) d t
$$


Step 3: Integrate both sides
Left-hand side:

$$
\int \frac{1}{y} d y=\ln |y|
$$


Right-hand side:

$$
\int 4 t\left(t^2+1\right) d t
$$


First expand the integrand:

$$
4 t\left(t^2+1\right)=4 t^3+4 t
$$


Now integrate:

$$
\int\left(4 t^3+4 t\right) d t=t^4+2 t^2+C
$$

Step 4: Combine results

$$
\ln |y|=t^4+2 t^2+C
$$


Exponentiate both sides:

$$
|y|=e^{t^4+2 t^2+C}=e^C \cdot e^{t^4+2 t^2}
$$


Let $A= \pm e^C$ (arbitrary nonzero constant), then the general solution is:

$$
y(t)=A e^{t^4+2 t^2}
$$


Also, note that $y(t)=0$ is not a solution, because it makes the original denominator zero. So we exclude $y=0$ from the solution set.

Final Answer:

$$
y(t)=A e^{t^4+2 t^2}=A e^{t^2\left(t^2+2\right)}, \text{with} A \in \mathbb{R}$, \quad A \in \mathbb{R} \backslash\{0\}
$$

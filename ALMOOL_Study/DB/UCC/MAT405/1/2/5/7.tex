Find the solutions to the IVP

$$
2 t y-y^{\prime}=0, \quad y(0)=3
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Rewrite the equation
Rearrange the equation to express it as a first-order differential equation:

$$
-y^{\prime}+2 t y=0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad y^{\prime}=2 t y
$$


This is a separable differential equation.

Step 2: Separate variables

$$
\frac{d y}{y}=2 t d t
$$


Step 3: Integrate both sides

$$
\begin{gathered}
\int \frac{1}{y} d y=\int 2 t d t \\
\ln |y|=t^2+C
\end{gathered}
$$


Exponentiate both sides:

$$
|y|=e^{t^2+C}=e^C \cdot e^{t^2}
$$


Let $A= \pm e^C$, then:

$$
y(t)=A e^{t^2}
$$


Step 4: Apply the initial condition $y(0)=3$

$$
y(0)=A e^0=A=3
$$


So $A=3$.

Final Answer:

$$
y(t)=3 e^{t^2}
$$

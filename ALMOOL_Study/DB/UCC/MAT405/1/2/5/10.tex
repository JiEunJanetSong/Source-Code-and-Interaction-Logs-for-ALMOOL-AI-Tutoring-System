Find all solutions of the equation

$$
y^{\prime}+t y=t y^2.
$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

This is a Bernoulli equation, which has the form:

$$
y^{\prime}+p(t) y=q(t) y^n
$$


In our case:

- $p(t)=t$

- $q(t)=t$

- $n=2$

Since $n \neq 0,1$, we use the standard substitution for Bernoulli equations.

Step 1: Divide both sides by $y^2$

We first write it in a clean Bernoulli form:

$$
y^{\prime}+t y=t y^2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad y^{\prime}=t y^2-t y
$$


But instead of dividing by $y^2$, we do the standard substitution:

Let

$$
v=y^{1-n}=y^{-1} \quad \Rightarrow \quad y=\frac{1}{v}
$$


Then use the chain rule:

$$
y^{\prime}=\frac{d}{d t}\left(\frac{1}{v}\right)=-\frac{v^{\prime}}{v^2}
$$


Substitute into the original equation:

$$
-\frac{v^{\prime}}{v^2}+t \cdot \frac{1}{v}=t \cdot \frac{1}{v^2}
$$


Multiply through by $v^2$ to eliminate denominators:

$$
-v^{\prime}+t v=t \quad \Rightarrow \quad v^{\prime}-t v=-t
$$

Step 2: Solve the linear ODE for $v(t)$

$$
v^{\prime}-t v=-t
$$


This is a first-order linear ODE. We use the integrating factor method.

Integrating factor:

$$
\mu(t)=e^{\int-t d t}=e^{-t^2 / 2}
$$


Multiply through:

$$
e^{-t^2 / 2} v^{\prime}-t e^{-t^2 / 2} v=-t e^{-t^2 / 2}
$$


Left-hand side is:

$$
\frac{d}{d t}\left(e^{-t^2 / 2} v\right)=-t e^{-t^2 / 2}
$$


Integrate both sides:

$$
\int \frac{d}{d t}\left(e^{-t^2 / 2} v\right) d t=\int-t e^{-t^2 / 2} d t
$$


Use substitution: Let $u=-t^2 / 2 \Rightarrow d u=-t d t$
Then:

$$
\int-t e^{-t^2 / 2} d t=\int e^u d u=e^u=e^{-t^2 / 2}+C
$$


So:

$$
e^{-t^2 / 2} v=e^{-t^2 / 2}+C \quad \Rightarrow \quad v=1+C e^{t^2 / 2}
$$


Step 3: Return to $y(t)$

Recall $v=\frac{1}{y}$, so:

$$
\frac{1}{y}=1+C e^{t^2 / 2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad y(t)=\frac{1}{1+C e^{t^2 / 2}}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
y(t)=\frac{1}{1+C e^{t^2 / 2}}, \quad C \in \mathbb{R}
$$

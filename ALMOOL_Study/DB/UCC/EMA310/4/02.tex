Find the vector product of $\underline{a}$ and $\underline{b}$ where

$$
\underline{a}=i+3 j+4 k \text { and } \underline{b}=2 i+7 j-5 k
$$




}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are asked to compute the vector product (also known as the cross product) of:

$$
\vec{a}=i+3 j+4 k, \quad \vec{b}=2 i+7 j-5 k
$$


Step 1: Use the determinant formula

The cross product $\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$ is given by:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b}=\left|\begin{array}{ccc}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
1 & 3 & 4 \\
2 & 7 & -5
\end{array}\right|
$$


Step 2: Compute the determinant

We expand this determinant:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b}=\mathbf{i}\left|\begin{array}{cc}
3 & 4 \\
7 & -5
\end{array}\right|-\mathbf{j}\left|\begin{array}{cc}
1 & 4 \\
2 & -5
\end{array}\right|+\mathbf{k}\left|\begin{array}{cc}
1 & 3 \\
2 & 7
\end{array}\right|
$$


Compute each $2 \times 2$ determinant:

- $\left|\begin{array}{cc}3 & 4 \\ 7 & -5\end{array}\right|=(3)(-5)-(4)(7)=-15-28=-43$

- $\left|\begin{array}{cc}1 & 4 \\ 2 & -5\end{array}\right|=(1)(-5)-(4)(2)=-5-8=-13$

- $\left|\begin{array}{ll}1 & 3 \\ 2 & 7\end{array}\right|=(1)(7)-(3)(2)=7-6=1$

Now plug back in:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b}=-43 \mathbf{i}+13 \mathbf{j}+1 \mathbf{k}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
\vec{a} \times \vec{b}=-43 i+13 j+k
$$

Find the area of the triangle with vertices $P(1,4,6), Q(-2,5,-1)$ and $R(1,-1,1)$.

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Compute two vectors from the triangle
Let's use vectors $\overrightarrow{P Q}$ and $\overrightarrow{P R}$ :

$$
\begin{gathered}
\overrightarrow{P Q}=Q-P=(-2-1,5-4,-1-6)=(-3,1,-7) \\
\overrightarrow{P R}=R-P=(1-1,-1-4,1-6)=(0,-5,-5)
\end{gathered}
$$


Step 2: Compute the cross product $\overrightarrow{P Q} \times \overrightarrow{P R}$
Use the determinant formula:

$$
\begin{gathered}
\overrightarrow{P Q} \times \overrightarrow{P R}=\left|\begin{array}{ccc}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
-3 & 1 & -7 \\
0 & -5 & -5
\end{array}\right| \\
=\mathbf{i}\left|\begin{array}{cc}
1 & -7 \\
-5 & -5
\end{array}\right|-\mathbf{j}\left|\begin{array}{cc}
-3 & -7 \\
0 & -5
\end{array}\right|+\mathbf{k}\left|\begin{array}{cc}
-3 & 1 \\
0 & -5
\end{array}\right|
\end{gathered}
$$


Compute each:
- $\left|\begin{array}{cc}1 & -7 \\ -5 & -5\end{array}\right|=(1)(-5)-(-7)(-5)=-5-35=-40$
- $\left|\begin{array}{cc}-3 & -7 \\ 0 & -5\end{array}\right|=(-3)(-5)-(-7)(0)=15-0=15$
- $\left|\begin{array}{cc}-3 & 1 \\ 0 & -5\end{array}\right|=(-3)(-5)-(1)(0)=15-0=15$

So:

$$
\overrightarrow{P Q} \times \overrightarrow{P R}=-40 \mathbf{i}-15 \mathbf{j}+15 \mathbf{k}
$$


Step 3: Find the magnitude of the cross product

$$
|\overrightarrow{P Q} \times \overrightarrow{P R}|=\sqrt{(-40)^2+(-15)^2+15^2}=\sqrt{1600+225+225}=\sqrt{2050}
$$


Step 4: Use the formula for area
The area $A$ of the triangle is:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& A=\frac{1}{2}|\overrightarrow{P Q} \times \overrightarrow{P R}|=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2050} \\
& =\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2050} \\
& =\frac{5\sqrt{82}}{2}  \text { units }^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Find the direction cosines of each of the following vectors

$$\underline{r}=4 i+12 j-5 k$$



}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find the direction cosines of a vector $\underline{r}=4 i+12 j-5 k$, we follow these steps:

Step 1: Compute the magnitude of $\underline{r}$

$$
|\underline{r}|=\sqrt{4^2+12^2+(-5)^2}=\sqrt{16+144+25}=\sqrt{185}
$$


Step 2: Compute the direction cosines
Direction cosines are the cosines of the angles between the vector and the coordinate axes, denoted as:

$$
\cos \alpha=\frac{r_x}{|\underline{x}|}, \quad \cos \beta=\frac{r_y}{|\underline{r}|}, \quad \cos \gamma=\frac{r_z}{|\underline{r}|}
$$


Where:

- $r_x=4$

- $r_y=12$

- $r_z=-5$

- $|\underline{r}|=\sqrt{185}$

So:

$$
\cos \alpha=\frac{4}{\sqrt{185}}, \quad \cos \beta=\frac{12}{\sqrt{185}}, \quad \cos \gamma=\frac{-5}{\sqrt{185}}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
\cos \alpha=\frac{4}{\sqrt{185}}, \quad \cos \beta=\frac{12}{\sqrt{185}}, \quad \cos \gamma=\frac{-5}{\sqrt{185}}
$$

Find the unit vector in the direction of each of the following vectors:

$$\underline{p}=-\frac{9}{5} i-8 j$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find the unit vector in the direction of a given vector $\underline{p}$, we divide the vector by its magnitude. The formula for the unit vector $\hat{p}$ is:

$$
\hat{p}=\frac{\underline{p}}{\|\underline{p}\|}
$$


The given vector is $\underline{p}=-\frac{9}{5} i-8 j$.

First, calculate the magnitude (or norm) of the vector $\underline{p}$ :

$$
\|\underline{p}\|=\sqrt{\left(-\frac{9}{5}\right)^2+(-8)^2}
$$

$\|\underline{p} \|=\sqrt{\frac{81}{25}+64}$

To add the terms under the square root, find acommon denominator (which is 25) :$\|p\|= \sqrt{\frac{81}{25}+\frac{64 \times 25}{25}}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \|\underline{p}\|=\sqrt{\frac{81}{25}+\frac{1600}{25}} \\
& \|\underline{p}\|=\sqrt{\frac{81+1600}{25}}
\end{aligned}
$$

$\|\underline{p}\|=\sqrt{\frac{1681}{25}}$ Now, take the square root of the numerator and the denominator $:\|p\|=\frac{\sqrt{1681}}{\sqrt{25}}$ 

We know $\sqrt{25}=5$. To find $\sqrt{1681}$, we can note that $40^2=1600$. Since 1681 ends in 1, its square root might end in 1 or 9. Let's check $41: 41^2=1681$. So, $\sqrt{1681}=41$.

$$
\|\underline{p}\|=\frac{41}{5}
$$


Now, divide the vector $\underline{p}$ by its magnitude $\frac{41}{5}$ :
$\$ \hat{p}=\frac{-\frac{9}{5} i-8 j}{\frac{41}{5}}$ To divide by a fraction, we multiply by its reciprocal $: \hat{p}=\left(-\frac{9}{5} i-8 j\right) \times \frac{5}{41}$
Distribute the multiplication : $\hat{p}=\left(-\frac{9}{5} \times \frac{5}{41}\right) i+\left(-8 \times \frac{5}{41}\right) j$ Simplify the coefficients : $\hat{p}=-\frac{9 \times 5}{5 \times 41} i-\frac{8 \times 5}{41} j \$$

$$
\hat{p}=-\frac{9}{41} i-\frac{40}{41} j
$$


So, the unit vector in the direction of $\underline{p}=-\frac{9}{5} i-8 j$ is $\hat{p}=-\frac{9}{41} i-\frac{40}{41} j$.

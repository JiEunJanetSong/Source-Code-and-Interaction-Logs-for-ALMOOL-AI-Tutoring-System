Find the unit vector in the direction of each of the following vectors:

$$\underline{a}=6 i+8 j$$



}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find the unit vector in the direction of a given vector $\underline{a}$, we need to divide the vector by its magnitude. The formula for the unit vector $\hat{a}$ is:

$$
\hat{a}=\frac{\underline{a}}{\|\underline{a}\|}
$$


The given vector is $\underline{a}=6 i+8 j$.
First, calculate the magnitude (or norm) of the vector $\underline{a}$ :

$$
\begin{gathered}
\|\underline{a}\|=\sqrt{6^2+8^2} \\
\|\underline{a}\|=\sqrt{36+64} \\
\|\underline{a}\|=\sqrt{100} \\
\|\underline{a}\|=10
\end{gathered}
$$


Now, divide the vector $\underline{a}$ by its magnitude 10:

$$
\hat{a}=\frac{6 i+8 j}{10}
$$


$$
\hat{a}=\frac{6}{10} i+\frac{8}{10} j
$$


Simplify the fractions:

$$
\hat{a}=\frac{3}{5} i+\frac{4}{5} j
$$


So, the unit vector in the direction of $\underline{a}=6 i+8 j$ is $\hat{a}=\frac{3}{5} i+\frac{4}{5} j$.

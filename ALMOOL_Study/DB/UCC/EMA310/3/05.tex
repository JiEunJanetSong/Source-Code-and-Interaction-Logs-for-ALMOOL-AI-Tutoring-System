Find the direction cosines of each of the following vectors

$$\underline{p}=i+2 j+3 k$$



}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find the direction cosines of the vector

$$
\underline{p}=i+2 j+3 k,
$$

follow these steps:

Step 1: Compute the magnitude of $p$

$$
|\underline{p}|=\sqrt{1^2+2^2+3^2}=\sqrt{1+4+9}=\sqrt{14}
$$


Step 2: Compute the direction cosines
Direction cosines are defined as:

$$
\cos \alpha=\frac{p_x}{|\underline{p}|}, \quad \cos \beta=\frac{p_y}{|\underline{p}|}, \quad \cos \gamma=\frac{p_z}{|\underline{p}|}
$$


Where:

- $p_x=1$

- $p_y=2$

- $p_z=3$

- $|\underline{p}|=\sqrt{14}$

So:

$$
\cos \alpha=\frac{1}{\sqrt{14}}, \quad \cos \beta=\frac{2}{\sqrt{14}}, \quad \cos \gamma=\frac{3}{\sqrt{14}}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
\cos \alpha=\frac{1}{\sqrt{14}}, \quad \cos \beta=\frac{2}{\sqrt{14}}, \quad \cos \gamma=\frac{3}{\sqrt{14}}
$$

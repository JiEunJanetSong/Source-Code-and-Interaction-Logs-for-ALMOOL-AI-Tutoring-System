Determine whether the function $y=x+1 / x$ is a solution of the given differential equation.

(a) $x y^{\prime}+y=2 x$

(b) $x y^{\prime \prime}+2 y^{\prime}=0$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

The first and second derivatives of $y=x+1 / x$ (with respect to $x$ ) are $y^{\prime}=1-1 / x^2$ and $y^{\prime \prime}=2 / x^3$.
(a) Substituting the expressions for $y$ and $y^{\prime}$ into the left side of the differential equation, we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
x y^{\prime}+y & =x\left(1-\frac{1}{x^2}\right)+\left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right) \\
& =x-\frac{1}{x}+x+\frac{1}{x}=2 x
\end{aligned}
$$


Because $2 x$ is equal to the right side of the differential equation, $y=x+1 / x$ is a solution.
(b) Substituting for $y^{\prime}$ and $y^{\prime \prime}$, the left side becomes

$$
\begin{aligned}
x y^{\prime \prime}+2 y^{\prime} & =x\left(\frac{2}{x^3}\right)+2\left(1-\frac{1}{x^2}\right) \\
& =\frac{2}{x^2}+2-\frac{2}{x^2}=2
\end{aligned}
$$

which is not equal to the right side of the differential equation. Thus $y=x+1 / x$ is not a solution.

Evaluate the integral.

$$\int_0^a \dfrac{d x}{\left(a^2+x^2\right)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}, \quad a>0$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

1. $x=a \tan (\theta)$. Then $d x=a \sec ^2(\theta) d \theta$.
2. $a^2+x^2=a^2+a^2 \tan ^2(\theta)=a^2 \sec ^2(\theta)$. So $\left(a^2+x^2\right)^{3 / 2}=a^3 \sec ^3(\theta)$.
3. Then

$$
\int_0^a \dfrac{d x}{\left(a^2+x^2\right)^{3 / 2}}=\int_{\theta=0}^{\theta=\pi / 4} \dfrac{a \sec ^2(\theta) d \theta}{a^3 \sec ^3(\theta)}=\int_0^{\pi / 4} \dfrac{1}{a^2} \dfrac{\sec ^2(\theta)}{\sec ^3(\theta)} d \theta=\int_0^{\pi / 4} \dfrac{1}{a^2} \sec ^{-1}(\theta) d \theta=\dfrac{1}{a^2} \int_0^{\pi / 4} \cos (\theta) d \theta
$$

4. $\int_0^{\pi / 4} \cos (\theta) d \theta=\left.\sin (\theta)\right|_0 ^{\pi / 4}=\sin (\pi / 4)-\sin (0)=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$.
5. Multiply by $\dfrac{1}{a^2}$ :

$$
\dfrac{1}{a^2} \cdot \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2 a^2}=\dfrac{1}{a^2 \sqrt{2}} .
$$


Exactly matching the result from the known antiderivative.
Hence the correct final answer is

$$
\int_0^a \dfrac{d x}{\left(a^2+x^2\right)^{3 / 2}}=\dfrac{1}{a^2 \sqrt{2}}, \quad a>0
$$

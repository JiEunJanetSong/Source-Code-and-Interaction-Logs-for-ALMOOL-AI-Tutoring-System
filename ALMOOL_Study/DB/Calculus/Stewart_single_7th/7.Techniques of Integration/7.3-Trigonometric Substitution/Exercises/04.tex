Evaluate the integral.

$$\int_0^1 x^3 \sqrt{1-x^2}\  d x$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int_0^1 x^3 \sqrt{1-x^2} d x
$$


A very direct method uses the substitution $x^2=s$. Let's go through the steps carefully.

1) Substitution

Set

$$
s=x^2 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad d s=2 x d x \quad \Longrightarrow \quad x d x=\dfrac{d s}{2} .
$$


Also, when $x$ goes from 0 to $1, s$ goes from 0 to 1.

We rewrite the integrand:

- $x^3=x \cdot x^2=x \cdot s$. But since $x=\sqrt{s}$, then $x^3=\sqrt{s} \cdot s=s^{3 / 2}$.

- $\sqrt{1-x^2}=\sqrt{1-s}$.
- $d x=\dfrac{d s}{2 x}=\dfrac{d s}{2 \sqrt{s}}$.

Hence,

$$
x^3 \sqrt{1-x^2} d x=\left[s^{3 / 2}\right] \sqrt{1-s}\left[\dfrac{d s}{2 \sqrt{s}}\right]
$$


Inside the bracket,

$$
s^{3 / 2} \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{s}}=s^{3 / 2-1 / 2}=s^1=s
$$


Therefore the expression becomes

$$
s \sqrt{1-s} \dfrac{1}{2} d s=\dfrac{1}{2} s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s
$$


So the integral transforms into

$$
\int_0^1 x^3 \sqrt{1-x^2} d x=\int_{s=0}^{s=1} \dfrac{1}{2} s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s=\dfrac{1}{2} \int_0^1 s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s
$$

2) Evaluate $\int_0^1 s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s$

Let's call this integral $I$ :

$$
I=\int_0^1 s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s
$$


One way is to expand and integrate term by term by using the substitution $u=1-s$. But an even quicker way is simply to do that substitution:

$$
u=1-s \quad \Longrightarrow \quad d u=-d s \quad \Longrightarrow \quad d s=-d u
$$

and when $s=0, u=1$; when $s=1, u=0$. 

Thus,

$$
I=\int_{s=0}^{s=1} s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s=\int_{u=1}^{u=0}[(1-u)](u)^{1 / 2}[-d u]
$$


Flipping the integration limits to avoid the negative sign:

$$
I=\int_0^1(1-u) u^{1 / 2} d u=\int_0^1\left[u^{1 / 2}-u^{3 / 2}\right] d u
$$


Split it:

$$
I=\int_0^1 u^{1 / 2} d u-\int_0^1 u^{3 / 2} d u
$$


Compute each:

1. $\int_0^1 u^{1 / 2} d u=\left[\dfrac{u^{3 / 2}}{\dfrac{3}{2}}\right]_0^1=\left[\dfrac{2}{3} u^{3 / 2}\right]_0^1=\dfrac{2}{3}$.

2. $\int_0^1 u^{3 / 2} d u=\left[\dfrac{u^{5 / 2}}{\dfrac{5}{2}}\right]_0^1=\left[\dfrac{2}{5} u^{5 / 2}\right]_0^1=\dfrac{2}{5}$.

So,

$$
I=\dfrac{2}{3}-\dfrac{2}{5}=\dfrac{10}{15}-\dfrac{6}{15}=\dfrac{4}{15}
$$


Therefore,

$$
\int_0^1 s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s=\dfrac{4}{15} .
$$

3) Combine with the factor $\dfrac{1}{2}$

Recall from the substitution step, we had a factor $\dfrac{1}{2}$ outside:

$$
\int_0^1 x^3 \sqrt{1-x^2} d x=\dfrac{1}{2} \int_0^1 s(1-s)^{1 / 2} d s=\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \dfrac{4}{15}=\dfrac{2}{15}.
$$


Hence, the final value of the definite integral is

$$
\int_0^1 x^3 \sqrt{1-x^2} d x=\dfrac{2}{15}
$$

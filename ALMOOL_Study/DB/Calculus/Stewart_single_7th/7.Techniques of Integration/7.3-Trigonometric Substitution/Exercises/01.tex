Evaluate the integral.

$$\int \dfrac{d x}{x^2 \sqrt{4-x^2}} $$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Use a trigonometric substitution
This form suggests the substitution:

$$
x=2 \sin \theta \quad \Rightarrow \quad d x=2 \cos \theta d \theta
$$


Also note:
- $\sqrt{4-x^2}=\sqrt{4-4 \sin ^2 \theta}=\sqrt{4 \cos ^2 \theta}=2 \cos \theta$
- $x^2=4 \sin ^2 \theta$

Step 2: Substitute into the integral

$$
\int \dfrac{d x}{x^2 \sqrt{4-x^2}}=\int \dfrac{2 \cos \theta}{4 \sin ^2 \theta \cdot 2 \cos \theta} d \theta=\int \dfrac{1}{4 \sin ^2 \theta} d \theta=\dfrac{1}{4} \int \csc ^2 \theta d \theta
$$


Step 3: Integrate

$$
\dfrac{1}{4} \int \csc ^2 \theta d \theta=-\dfrac{1}{4} \cot \theta+C
$$


Step 4: Back-substitute
Recall:

$$
x=2 \sin \theta \Rightarrow \sin \theta=\dfrac{x}{2}
$$


Then:

$$
\cot \theta=\dfrac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta}=\dfrac{\sqrt{1-\sin ^2 \theta}}{\sin \theta}=\dfrac{\sqrt{1-\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right)^2}}{\dfrac{x}{2}}=\dfrac{\sqrt{4-x^2}}{x}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
-\dfrac{1}{4} \cdot \dfrac{\sqrt{4-x^2}}{x}+C
$$

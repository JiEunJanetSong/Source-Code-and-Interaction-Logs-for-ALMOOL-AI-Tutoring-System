Evaluate the integral.

$$\int \dfrac{t^5}{\sqrt{t^2+2}}\  d t$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int \dfrac{t^5}{\sqrt{t^2+2}} d t
$$


A good strategy is to use the substitution

$$
u=t^2+2
$$

so that

$$
d u=2 t d t \quad \Longrightarrow \quad t d t=\dfrac{d u}{2} .
$$


Notice that $t^5 d t$ can be written as $t^4(t d t)$. Since $u=t^2+2$, we have

$$
t^2=u-2 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad t^4=\left(t^2\right)^2=(u-2)^2
$$


Also, the square root in the denominator becomes

$$
\sqrt{t^2+2}=\sqrt{u}
$$


Substituting all of this into the integral, we get

$$
\int \dfrac{t^5}{\sqrt{t^2+2}} d t=\int \dfrac{(u-2)^2}{\sqrt{u}} \cdot \dfrac{d u}{2}=\dfrac{1}{2} \int \dfrac{(u-2)^2}{u^{1 / 2}} d u.
$$


Now, expand the numerator:

$$
(u-2)^2=u^2-4 u+4
$$

Then, the integrand becomes

$$
\dfrac{u^2-4 u+4}{u^{1 / 2}}=u^{3 / 2}-4 u^{1 / 2}+4 u^{-1 / 2}
$$


Thus, the integral is

$$
\dfrac{1}{2} \int\left(u^{3 / 2}-4 u^{1 / 2}+4 u^{-1 / 2}\right) d u
$$


Now, integrate term by term:
1. $\int u^{3 / 2} d u=\dfrac{2}{5} u^{5 / 2}$,
2. $\int u^{1 / 2} d u=\dfrac{2}{3} u^{3 / 2}$,
3. $\int u^{-1 / 2} d u=2 u^{1 / 2}$.

Putting these together:

$$
\dfrac{1}{2}\left[\dfrac{2}{5} u^{5 / 2}-4\left(\dfrac{2}{3} u^{3 / 2}\right)+4\left(2 u^{1 / 2}\right)\right]+C
$$


Simplify each term:
- The first term: $\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \dfrac{2}{5} u^{5 / 2}=\dfrac{1}{5} u^{5 / 2}$.
- The second term: $\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 4 \cdot \dfrac{2}{3} u^{3 / 2}=\dfrac{8}{6} u^{3 / 2}=\dfrac{4}{3} u^{3 / 2}$ (and note the minus sign gives $-\dfrac{4}{3} u^{3 / 2}$ ).
- The third term: $\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 4 \cdot 2 u^{1 / 2}=4 u^{1 / 2}$.

Thus, the antiderivative in terms of $u$ is

$$
\dfrac{1}{5} u^{5 / 2}-\dfrac{4}{3} u^{3 / 2}+4 u^{1 / 2}+C
$$


Finally, substitute back $u=t^2+2$ :

$$
\dfrac{1}{5}\left(t^2+2\right)^{5 / 2}-\dfrac{4}{3}\left(t^2+2\right)^{3 / 2}+4\left(t^2+2\right)^{1 / 2}+C
$$

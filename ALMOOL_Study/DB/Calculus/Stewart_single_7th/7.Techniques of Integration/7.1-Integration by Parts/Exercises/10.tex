Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ \sin^{-1} \ x \ d x$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate the integral

$$
\int \sin ^{-1}(x) d x
$$

where $\sin ^{-1}(x)$ is the inverse sine (arcsine) function, often written as $\arcsin (x)$.

Method: Integration by Parts
Recall the integration by parts formula:

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$

1. Choose $u$ and $d v$ :

Let

$$
u=\arcsin (x), \quad d v=d x
$$

2. Compute $d u$ and $v$ :
- $d u$ is the derivative of $\arcsin (x)$ :

$$
d u=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} d x
$$

- $v$ is the antiderivative of $d x$ :

$$
v=\int d x=x
$$

3. Apply the formula:

$$
\int \arcsin (x) d x=u v-\int v d u=x \arcsin (x)-\int x \cdot \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} d x
$$

4. Compute the remaining integral:

$$
\int x \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} d x
$$


We can use a simple substitution here. Let

$$
w=1-x^2 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad d w=-2 x d x \quad \Longrightarrow \quad x d x=-\dfrac{1}{2} d w .
$$


So

$$
\int x \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} d x=\int \dfrac{x d x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}=\int \dfrac{-\dfrac{1}{2} d w}{\sqrt{w}}=-\dfrac{1}{2} \int w^{-\dfrac{1}{2}} d w=-\dfrac{1}{2}\left(2 w^{\dfrac{1}{2}}\right)=-\sqrt{w}=-\sqrt{1-x^2}
$$


Therefore,

$$
\int x \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} d x=-\sqrt{1-x^2}
$$

5. Put it all back together:

$$
\int \arcsin (x) d x=x \arcsin (x)-\left(-\sqrt{1-x^2}\right)=x \arcsin (x)+\sqrt{1-x^2}+C
$$

Final Result

$$
\int \sin ^{-1}(x) d x=x \sin ^{-1}(x)+\sqrt{1-x^2}+C .
$$

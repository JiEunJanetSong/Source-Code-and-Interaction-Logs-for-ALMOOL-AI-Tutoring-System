Evaluate the integral using integration by parts with the indicated choices of $u$ and $dv$.
   
$$\int\ \theta\ \cos \ \theta\ d\theta;\ u=\theta,\ dv=\cos\ \theta\ d\theta$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Using the formula for integration by parts,

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$

we make the choices $u=\theta$ and $d v=\cos (\theta) d \theta$.
1. Compute $d u$ and $v$ :

$$
d u=d \theta, \quad v=\int \cos (\theta) d \theta=\sin (\theta)
$$

2. Substitute into the integration by parts formula:

$$
\int \theta \cos (\theta) d \theta=\theta \sin (\theta)-\int \sin (\theta) d \theta
$$

3. Evaluate the remaining integral:

$$
\int \sin (\theta) d \theta=-\cos (\theta)
$$

4. Combine results:

$$
\int \theta \cos (\theta) d \theta=\theta \sin (\theta)-(-\cos (\theta))=\theta \sin (\theta)+\cos (\theta)+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.

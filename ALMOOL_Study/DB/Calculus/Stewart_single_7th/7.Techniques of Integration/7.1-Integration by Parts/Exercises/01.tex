Evaluate the integral using integration by parts with the indicated choices of $u$ and $dv$.
   
$$\int\ x^2\ \ln \ x\ dx \quad u=\ln\ x,\ du=x^2\ dx$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Differentiate $u$ and integrate $d v$

1. If $u=\ln x$, then

$$
d u=\dfrac{1}{x} d x
$$

2. If $d v=x^2 d x$, then

$$
v=\int x^2 d x=\dfrac{x^3}{3}
$$


Step 2: Apply the integration-by-parts formula
Recall the formula:

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$


Hence,

$$
\int x^2 \ln x d x=(\ln x)\left(\dfrac{x^3}{3}\right)-\int\left(\dfrac{x^3}{3}\right)\left(\dfrac{1}{x}\right) d x
$$


Simplify inside the integral:

$$
=\dfrac{x^3}{3} \ln x-\int \dfrac{x^3}{3} \cdot \dfrac{1}{x} d x=\dfrac{x^3}{3} \ln x-\int \dfrac{x^2}{3} d x
$$


Step 3: Evaluate the remaining integral

$$
\int \dfrac{x^2}{3} d x=\dfrac{1}{3} \int x^2 d x=\dfrac{1}{3} \cdot \dfrac{x^3}{3}=\dfrac{x^3}{9}
$$


Thus,

$$
\int x^2 \ln x d x=\dfrac{x^3}{3} \ln x-\dfrac{x^3}{9}+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.

Final Answer

$$
\int x^2 \ln x d x=\dfrac{x^3}{3} \ln x-\dfrac{x^3}{9}+C.
$$

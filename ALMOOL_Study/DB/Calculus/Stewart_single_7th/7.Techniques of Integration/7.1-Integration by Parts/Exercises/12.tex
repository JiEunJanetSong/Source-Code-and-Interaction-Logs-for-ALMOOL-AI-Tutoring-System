Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ p^5 \ \ln\ p \ d p$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int p^5 \ln (p) d p
$$


A convenient way is to use integration by parts. Recall the formula:

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$


Step 1: Choose $u$ and $d v$

Let

$$
u=\ln (p), \quad d v=p^5 d p
$$


Then

$$
d u=\dfrac{1}{p} d p, \quad v=\int p^5 d p=\dfrac{p^6}{6}
$$

Step 2: Apply integration by parts

$$
\int p^5 \ln (p) d p=\ln (p) \cdot \dfrac{p^6}{6}-\int \dfrac{p^6}{6} \cdot \dfrac{1}{p} d p
$$


Simplify the second integral:

$$
\int \dfrac{p^6}{6} \cdot \dfrac{1}{p} d p=\dfrac{1}{6} \int p^5 d p=\dfrac{1}{6} \cdot \dfrac{p^6}{6}=\dfrac{p^6}{36}
$$


Thus,

$$
\int p^5 \ln (p) d p=\dfrac{p^6}{6} \ln (p)-\dfrac{p^6}{36}+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.

Final Answer

$$
\int p^5 \ln (p) d p=\dfrac{p^6}{6} \ln (p)-\dfrac{p^6}{36}+C
$$

Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ e^{-\theta} \ \cos \ 2\theta\ d \theta$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

A convenient shortcut for integrals of the form $\int e^{a \theta} \cos (b \theta) d \theta$ is the known result:

$$
\int e^{a \theta} \cos (b \theta) d \theta=\dfrac{e^{a \theta}}{a^2+b^2}(a \cos (b \theta)+b \sin (b \theta))+C
$$


In your case, $a=-1$ and $b=2$. Then $a^2+b^2=1+4=5$, and substituting into the formula gives:

$$
\int e^{-\theta} \cos (2 \theta) d \theta=\dfrac{e^{-\theta}}{5}((-1) \cos (2 \theta)+2 \sin (2 \theta))+C
$$


Simplifying the factor:

$$
\int e^{-\theta} \cos (2 \theta) d \theta=\dfrac{e^{-\theta}}{5}(-\cos (2 \theta)+2 \sin (2 \theta))+C .
$$

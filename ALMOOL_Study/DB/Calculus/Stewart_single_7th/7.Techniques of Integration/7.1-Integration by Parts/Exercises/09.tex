Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ \ln \ \sqrt[3]{x} \ d x$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

First, rewrite the integrand:

$$
\ln (\sqrt[3]{x})=\ln \left(x^{\frac{1}{3}}\right)=\dfrac{1}{3} \ln (x)
$$


So, the integral becomes:

$$
\int \ln (\sqrt[3]{x}) d x=\int \dfrac{1}{3} \ln (x) d x=\dfrac{1}{3} \int \ln (x) d x
$$


A known standard result is:

$$
\int \ln (x) d x=x \ln (x)-x+C
$$


Hence,

$$
\dfrac{1}{3} \int \ln (x) d x=\dfrac{1}{3}(x \ln (x)-x)+C=\dfrac{x}{3} \ln (x)-\dfrac{x}{3}+C .
$$


Therefore,

$$
\int \ln (\sqrt[3]{x}) d x=\dfrac{x}{3} \ln (x)-\dfrac{x}{3}+C
$$

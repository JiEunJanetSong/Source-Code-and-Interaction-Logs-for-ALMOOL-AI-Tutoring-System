Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ \arctan \ 4\ t \ d t$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int \arctan (4 t) d t
$$


A convenient method is integration by parts. Recall that

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$


Step 1: Choose $u$ and $d v$
Let

$$
u=\arctan (4 t), \quad d v=d t
$$


Then

$$
d u=\dfrac{4}{1+(4 t)^2} d t=\dfrac{4}{1+16 t^2} d t, \quad v=\int d t=t
$$


Step 2: Apply the formula

$$
\int \arctan (4 t) d t=u v-\int v d u=t \arctan (4 t)-\int t\left(\dfrac{4}{1+16 t^2}\right) d t
$$

Hence,

$$
\int \arctan (4 t) d t=t \arctan (4 t)-4 \int \dfrac{t}{1+16 t^2} d t
$$


Step 3: Integrate $\int \dfrac{t}{1+16 t^2} d t$
Use the substitution:

$$
u=1+16 t^2 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad d u=32 t d t \quad \Longrightarrow \quad t d t=\dfrac{d u}{32}
$$


Thus,

$$
\int \dfrac{t}{1+16 t^2} d t=\int \dfrac{1}{u} \dfrac{d u}{32}=\dfrac{1}{32} \int \dfrac{d u}{u}=\dfrac{1}{32} \ln |u|+C=\dfrac{1}{32} \ln \left(1+16 t^2\right)+C
$$

(Since $1+16 t^2>0$ for all real $t$, we don't strictly need the absolute value.)
Hence,

$$
4 \int \dfrac{t}{1+16 t^2} d t=4\left(\dfrac{1}{32} \ln \left(1+16 t^2\right)\right)=\dfrac{4}{32} \ln \left(1+16 t^2\right)=\dfrac{1}{8} \ln \left(1+16 t^2\right)
$$

Step 4: Combine everything

$$
\int \arctan (4 t) d t=t \arctan (4 t)-\left[\dfrac{1}{8} \ln \left(1+16 t^2\right)\right]+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.

Final Answer

$$
\int \arctan (4 t) d t=t \arctan (4 t)-\dfrac{1}{8} \ln \left(1+16 t^2\right)+C .
$$

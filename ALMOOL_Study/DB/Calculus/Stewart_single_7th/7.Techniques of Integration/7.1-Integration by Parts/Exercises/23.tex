Evaluate the integral.

$$\int^\tfrac{1}{2}_{0}\ x\ \cos\ \pi x\ d x$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To evaluate

$$
\int_0^{\dfrac{1}{2}} x \cos (\pi x) d x
$$

we can first find the indefinite integral $\int x \cos (\pi x) d x$ using integration by parts and then apply the limits 0 to $\dfrac{1}{2}$.

1) Indefinite integral $\int x \cos (\pi x) d x$

Use integration by parts:

- Let $u=x$. Then $d u=d x$.

- Let $d v=\cos (\pi x) d x$. 

Then

$$
v=\int \cos (\pi x) d x=\dfrac{1}{\pi} \sin (\pi x)
$$


Applying the formula $\int u d v=u v-\int v d u$, we get

$$
\int x \cos (\pi x) d x=x \cdot \dfrac{1}{\pi} \sin (\pi x)-\int \dfrac{1}{\pi} \sin (\pi x) d x
$$


The remaining integral is

$$
\int \sin (\pi x) d x=-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)
$$

Hence,

$$
-\int \dfrac{1}{\pi} \sin (\pi x) d x=-\dfrac{1}{\pi}\left(-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)\right)=\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \cos (\pi x)
$$


Putting it all together:

$$
\int x \cos (\pi x) d x=\dfrac{x}{\pi} \sin (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \cos (\pi x)+C
$$

2) Evaluate the definite integral from 0 to $\dfrac{1}{2}$

We have

$$
\int_0^{\dfrac{1}{2}} x \cos (\pi x) d x=\left[\dfrac{x}{\pi} \sin (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \cos (\pi x)\right]_0^{1 / 2}
$$


At $x=\dfrac{1}{2}$ :

- $\sin \left(\pi \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}\right)=\sin \left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)=1$.

- $\cos \left(\pi \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}\right)=\cos \left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)=0$.

So the expression becomes

$$
\dfrac{\dfrac{1}{2}}{\pi} \cdot 1+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \cdot 0=\dfrac{1}{2 \pi}
$$

At $x=0$ :

- $\sin (0)=0$.

- $\cos (0)=1$.

So the expression becomes

$$
\dfrac{0}{\pi} \cdot 0+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \cdot 1=\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} .
$$


Subtracting,

$$
\left[\dfrac{x}{\pi} \sin (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \cos (\pi x)\right]_0^{1 / 2}=\left(\dfrac{1}{2 \pi}\right)-\left(\dfrac{1}{\pi^2}\right)
$$


We can leave the result in this form or combine it into a single fraction:

$$
\dfrac{1}{2 \pi}-\dfrac{1}{\pi^2}=\dfrac{\pi}{2 \pi^2}-\dfrac{1}{\pi^2}=\dfrac{\pi-2}{2 \pi^2}
$$


Both forms are correct. A compact final answer is

$$
\int_0^{\dfrac{1}{2}} x \cos (\pi x) d x=\dfrac{1}{2 \pi}-\dfrac{1}{\pi^2}=\dfrac{\pi-2}{2 \pi^2}
$$

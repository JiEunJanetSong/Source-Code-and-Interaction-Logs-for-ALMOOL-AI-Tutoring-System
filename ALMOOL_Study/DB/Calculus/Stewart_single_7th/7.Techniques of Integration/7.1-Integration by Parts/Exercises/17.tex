Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ e^{2\theta} \ \sin \ 3\theta\ d \theta$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

A quick way to evaluate integrals of the form

$$
\int e^{a \theta} \sin (b \theta) d \theta \text { or } \int e^{a \theta} \cos (b \theta) d \theta
$$

is to use a known result:

$$
\int e^{a \theta} \sin (b \theta) d \theta=\dfrac{e^{a \theta}}{a^2+b^2}(a \sin (b \theta)-b \cos (b \theta))+C .
$$


In this problem, we have $a=2$ and $b=3$. Hence

$$
\int e^{2 \theta} \sin (3 \theta) d \theta=\dfrac{e^{2 \theta}}{2^2+3^2}(2 \sin (3 \theta)-3 \cos (3 \theta))+C .
$$


Since $2^2+3^2=4+9=13$, the final answer is

$$
\int e^{2 \theta} \sin (3 \theta) d \theta=\dfrac{e^{2 \theta}}{13}(2 \sin (3 \theta)-3 \cos (3 \theta))+C .
$$

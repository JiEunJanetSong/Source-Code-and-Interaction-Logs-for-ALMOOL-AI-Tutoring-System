Evaluate the integral.
   
$$\int\ x\ \tan^2\ x\ d x$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int x \tan ^2(x) d x .
$$


A helpful identity is

$$
\tan ^2(x)=\sec ^2(x)-1
$$


Thus,

$$
\int x \tan ^2(x) d x=\int x\left(\sec ^2(x)-1\right) d x=\int x \sec ^2(x) d x-\int x d x
$$

1. Handle $\int x \sec ^2(x) d x$

Use integration by parts:

$$
u=x, \quad d v=\sec ^2(x) d x \Longrightarrow d u=d x, \quad v=\tan (x)
$$


Then

$$
\int x \sec ^2(x) d x=x \tan (x)-\int \tan (x) d x
$$


We know

$$
\int \tan (x) d x=-\ln |\cos (x)|
$$

Hence,

$$
\int x \sec ^2(x) d x=x \tan (x)+\ln |\cos (x)|
$$

2. Handle $\int x d x$

This is straightforward:

$$
\int x d x=\dfrac{x^2}{2}
$$


Putting them together,

$$
\int x \tan ^2(x) d x=(x \tan (x)+\ln |\cos (x)|)-\dfrac{x^2}{2}+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.
Therefore, a concise final answer is:

$$
\int x \tan ^2(x) d x=x \tan (x)+\ln |\cos (x)|-\dfrac{x^2}{2}+C.
$$

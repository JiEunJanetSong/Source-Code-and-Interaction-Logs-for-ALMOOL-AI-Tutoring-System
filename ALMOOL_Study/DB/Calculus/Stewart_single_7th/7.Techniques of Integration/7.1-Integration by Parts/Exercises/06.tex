Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ (x-1)\ \sin \ \pi x\ d x$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int(x-1) \sin (\pi x) d x
$$


A convenient approach is to split the integral into two separate integrals:

$$
\int(x-1) \sin (\pi x) d x=\int x \sin (\pi x) d x-\int \sin (\pi x) d x
$$


We handle each part separately.
1) Integrate $\int x \sin (\pi x) d x$

Use integration by parts:
- Let $u=x$. Then $d u=d x$.
- Let $d v=\sin (\pi x) d x$. Then

$$
v=\int \sin (\pi x) d x=-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)
$$


Using the formula $\int u d v=u v-\int v d u$, we get:

$$
\int x \sin (\pi x) d x=x\left(-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)\right)-\int\left(-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)\right) d x
$$


Simplify step by step:
1. First term:

$$
x \cdot\left(-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)\right)=-\dfrac{x}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)
$$

2. Second term:

$$
-\int\left(-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)\right) d x=\dfrac{1}{\pi} \int \cos (\pi x) d x=\dfrac{1}{\pi}\left(\dfrac{1}{\pi} \sin (\pi x)\right)=\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \sin (\pi x)
$$


Hence,

$$
\int x \sin (\pi x) d x=-\dfrac{x}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \sin (\pi x)
$$

2) Integrate $\int \sin (\pi x) d x$

This is simpler:

$$
\int \sin (\pi x) d x=-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x) .
$$

3) Combine both parts

$$
\int(x-1) \sin (\pi x) d x=\int x \sin (\pi x) d x-\int \sin (\pi x) d x .
$$


Substitute the results:

$$
=\left[-\dfrac{x}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \sin (\pi x)\right]-\left[-\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)\right] .
$$


Distribute the minus sign in the second bracket:

$$
=-\dfrac{x}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \sin (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x) .
$$


Combine like terms involving $\cos (\pi x)$ :

$$
-\dfrac{x}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)=-\dfrac{x-1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x) .
$$


Hence the integral is

$$
\int(x-1) \sin (\pi x) d x=-\dfrac{x-1}{\pi} \cos (\pi x)+\dfrac{1}{\pi^2} \sin (\pi x)+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.

Evaluate the integral.
   
$$\int\ x\ \cos \ 5x\ d x$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To integrate

$$
\int x \cos (5 x) d x
$$

we use integration by parts. Let

$$
u=x \quad \text { and } \quad d v=\cos (5 x) d x .
$$


Then,

$$
d u=d x \quad \text { and } \quad v=\int \cos (5 x) d x=\dfrac{1}{5} \sin (5 x)
$$


Substituting into the integration by parts formula,

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$

we get:

$$
\int x \cos (5 x) d x=x \cdot \dfrac{1}{5} \sin (5 x)-\int \dfrac{1}{5} \sin (5 x) d x
$$


Next, integrate $\dfrac{1}{5} \sin (5 x)$ :

$$
\int \dfrac{1}{5} \sin (5 x) d x=\dfrac{1}{5} \int \sin (5 x) d x=\dfrac{1}{5}\left(-\dfrac{1}{5} \cos (5 x)\right)=-\dfrac{1}{25} \cos (5 x)
$$


Putting this all together:

$$
\int x \cos (5 x) d x=\dfrac{x}{5} \sin (5 x)-\left(-\dfrac{1}{25} \cos (5 x)\right)=\dfrac{x}{5} \sin (5 x)+\dfrac{1}{25} \cos (5 x)+C
$$

where $C$ is the constant of integration.

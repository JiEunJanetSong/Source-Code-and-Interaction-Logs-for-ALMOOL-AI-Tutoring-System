Evaluate the integral.

$$\int\ t\ \sec^2 \ 2\ t \ d t$$

}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to evaluate

$$
\int t \sec ^2(2 t) d t
$$


A suitable approach is integration by parts. Recall the formula:

$$
\int u d v=u v-\int v d u
$$

1) Set up integration by parts

Let

$$
u=t, \quad d v=\sec ^2(2 t) d t
$$


Then:

$$
d u=d t, \quad v=\int \sec ^2(2 t) d t
$$


We know that $\dfrac{d}{d t} \tan (2 t)=2 \sec ^2(2 t)$. Hence

$$
\int \sec ^2(2 t) d t=\dfrac{1}{2} \tan (2 t)
$$

2) Apply the integration by parts formula

$$
\int t \sec ^2(2 t) d t=u v-\int v d u=t \cdot\left(\dfrac{1}{2} \tan (2 t)\right)-\int\left(\dfrac{1}{2} \tan (2 t)\right) d t
$$


So

$$
\int t \sec ^2(2 t) d t=\dfrac{t}{2} \tan (2 t)-\dfrac{1}{2} \int \tan (2 t) d t
$$

3) Handle the remaining integral $\int \tan (2 t) d t$

A known standard formula is:

$$
\int \tan (a x) d x=-\dfrac{1}{a} \ln |\cos (a x)|+C
$$


Thus,

$$
\int \tan (2 t) d t=-\dfrac{1}{2} \ln |\cos (2 t)|+C
$$

4) Combine the results

Substitute back:

$$
\int t \sec ^2(2 t) d t=\dfrac{t}{2} \tan (2 t)-\dfrac{1}{2}\left(-\dfrac{1}{2} \ln |\cos (2 t)|\right)+C .
$$


Distribute:

$$
=\dfrac{t}{2} \tan (2 t)+\dfrac{1}{4} \ln |\cos (2 t)|+C .
$$


Hence, the final result is:

$$
\int t \sec ^2(2 t) d t=\dfrac{t}{2} \tan (2 t)+\dfrac{1}{4} \ln |\cos (2 t)|+C .
$$

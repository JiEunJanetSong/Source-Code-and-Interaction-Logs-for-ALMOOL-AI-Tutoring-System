Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\left( 1+\frac{2}{n} \right) ^n$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \left(1 + \frac{2}{n} \right)^n
$

We want to determine whether this sequence converges or diverges, and if it converges, find the limit as $n \to \infty$.



 Step 1: Recognize a standard exponential limit form

This is a classic exponential limit of the form:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{k}{n} \right)^n = e^k
$

In our case, $k = 2$, so:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{2}{n} \right)^n = e^2
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{e^2}
$

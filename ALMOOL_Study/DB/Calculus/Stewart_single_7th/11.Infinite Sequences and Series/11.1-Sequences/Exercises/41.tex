Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$\left\{ n^2e^{-n} \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = n^2 e^{-n}
$

We want to determine whether it converges or diverges as $n \to \infty$, and if it converges, find the limit.



 Step 1: Understand the growth of terms

- $n^2 \to \infty$ (polynomial growth)

- $e^{-n} \to 0$ (exponential decay)

So the sequence is the product of a polynomial term and a rapidly decaying exponential term.


 Step 2: Use limit comparison or known result

A classic result is that for any positive integer $k$, the sequence:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{n^k}{e^n} = 0
$

This means exponential decay dominates polynomial growth as $n \to \infty$.

In our case, $a_n = \frac{n^2}{e^n}$, so:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{n^2}{e^n} = 0
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\cos \left(\frac{2}{n} \right) $$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \cos\left( \frac{2}{n} \right)
$

Step 1: Analyze the Behavior of the Argument

As $n \to \infty$, we know:

$
\frac{2}{n} \to 0
$

Step 2: Use Continuity of Cosine

Since $\cos(x)$ is continuous, we can take the limit inside the function:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \cos\left( \frac{2}{n} \right) = \cos(0) = 1
$

Final Answer:

- The sequence converges

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{1}
$

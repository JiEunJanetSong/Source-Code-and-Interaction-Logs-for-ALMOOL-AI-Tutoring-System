Calculate, to four decimal places, the first ten terms of the sequence and use them to plot the graph of the sequence by hand. Does the sequence appear to have a limit? If so, calculate it. If not, explain why.

$$a_n=1+\left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^n$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^n
$

 Step 1: Calculate the First 10 Terms (to 4 decimal places)

We compute $a_n$ for $n = 1$ to $10$:

$
\begin{align}
a_1 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^1 = 1 - 0.5 = 0.5000 \\
a_2 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^2 = 1 + 0.25 = 1.2500 \\
a_3 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^3 = 1 - 0.125 = 0.8750 \\
a_4 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^4 = 1 + 0.0625 = 1.0625 \\
a_5 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^5 = 1 - 0.03125 = 0.9688 \\
a_6 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^6 = 1 + 0.015625 = 1.0156 \\
a_7 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^7 = 1 - 0.0078125 = 0.9922 \\
a_8 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^8 = 1 + 0.00390625 = 1.0039 \\
a_9 &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^9 = 1 - 0.001953125 = 0.9980 \\
a_{10} &= 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^{10} = 1 + 0.0009765625 = 1.0010 \\
\end{align}
$

 Step 2: Plot by Hand (Description)

To sketch the graph:

- Plot points $(n, a_n)$ for $n = 1$ through $10$

- You'll notice:

  - The sequence oscillates above and below 1

  - The amount it oscillates gets smaller over time

  - The values get closer to 1

 Step 3: Does the Sequence Have a Limit?

The sequence is:

$
a_n = 1 + \left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^n
$

As $n \to \infty$, the term $\left( -\frac{1}{2} \right)^n \to 0$  
(since itâ€™s a geometric sequence with common ratio $r = -\frac{1}{2}$, and $|r| < 1$)

So:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 1 + 0 = 1
$

 Final Answer:

- First 10 terms (4 decimal places):  
  $
  \boxed{0.5000,\ 1.2500,\ 0.8750,\ 1.0625,\ 0.9688,\ 1.0156,\ 0.9922,\ 1.0039,\ 0.9980,\ 1.0010}
  $

- The sequence has a limit, and the limit is:  
  $
  \boxed{1}
  $

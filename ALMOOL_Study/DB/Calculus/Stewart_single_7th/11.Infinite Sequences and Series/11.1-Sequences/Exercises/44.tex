Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\sqrt[n]{2^{1+3n}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \sqrt[n]{2^{1 + 3n}} = \left(2^{1 + 3n}\right)^{1/n}
$



 Step 1: Simplify the expression

Using the exponent rule $(a^b)^c = a^{bc}$, we get:

$
a_n = 2^{\frac{1 + 3n}{n}} = 2^{\frac{1}{n} + 3}
$



 Step 2: Take the limit as $n \to \infty$

We know:

- $\frac{1}{n} \to 0$

So:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} 2^{\frac{1}{n} + 3} = 2^3 = 8
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{8}
$

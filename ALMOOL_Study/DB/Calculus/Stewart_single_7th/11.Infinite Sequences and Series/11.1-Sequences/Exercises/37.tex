Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$\left\{ \frac{\left( 2n-1 \right) !}{\left( 2n+1 \right) !} \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{(2n - 1)!}{(2n + 1)!}
$

Step 1: Expand the Denominator

We know that:

$
(2n + 1)! = (2n + 1)(2n)(2n - 1)!
$

So we can write:

$
a_n = \frac{(2n - 1)!}{(2n + 1)(2n)(2n - 1)!}
$

Cancel $(2n - 1)!$ from numerator and denominator:

$
a_n = \frac{1}{(2n)(2n + 1)}
$

Step 2: Take the Limit as $n \to \infty$

$
a_n = \frac{1}{(2n)(2n + 1)} \to 0 \quad \text{as } n \to \infty
$

Because the denominator grows without bound, the fraction tends to 0.

Final Answer:

- The sequence converges

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

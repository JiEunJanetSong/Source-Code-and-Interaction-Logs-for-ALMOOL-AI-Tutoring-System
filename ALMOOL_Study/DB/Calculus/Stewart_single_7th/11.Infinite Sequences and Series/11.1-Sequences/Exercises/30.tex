Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\sqrt{\frac{n+1}{9n+1}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \sqrt{\frac{n+1}{9n+1}}
$

 Step 1: Analyze the Inside of the Square Root

We simplify the fraction inside the square root:

$
\frac{n+1}{9n+1}
$

Divide numerator and denominator by $n$:

$
\frac{n(1 + \frac{1}{n})}{n(9 + \frac{1}{n})} = \frac{1 + \frac{1}{n}}{9 + \frac{1}{n}}
$

As $n \to \infty$, we get:

$
\frac{1 + \frac{1}{n}}{9 + \frac{1}{n}} \to \frac{1}{9}
$

 Step 2: Take the Square Root

$
a_n = \sqrt{\frac{n+1}{9n+1}} \to \sqrt{\frac{1}{9}} = \frac{1}{3}
$

 Final Answer:

- The sequence converges

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{\frac{1}{3}}
$

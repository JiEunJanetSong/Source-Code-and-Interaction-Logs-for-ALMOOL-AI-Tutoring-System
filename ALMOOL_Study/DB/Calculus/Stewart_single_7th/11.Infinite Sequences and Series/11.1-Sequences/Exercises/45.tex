Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=n\sin\left( \frac{1}{n} \right) $$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = n \sin\left( \frac{1}{n} \right)
$



 Step 1: Use a known limit identity

There is a standard limit result:

$
\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1
$

Let $x = \frac{1}{n}$. Then as $n \to \infty$, $x \to 0$, so:

$
\sin\left(\frac{1}{n}\right) = \frac{1}{n} \cdot \left(1 + o(1)\right)
$

Multiplying by $n$:

$
a_n = n \cdot \sin\left(\frac{1}{n}\right) \approx n \cdot \frac{1}{n} = 1
$

More rigorously:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} n \sin\left(\frac{1}{n}\right) = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{1}
$

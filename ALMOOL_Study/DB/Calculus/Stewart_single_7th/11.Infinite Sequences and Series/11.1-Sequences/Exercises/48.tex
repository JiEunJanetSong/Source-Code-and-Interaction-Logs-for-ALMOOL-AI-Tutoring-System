Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{\sin 2n}{1+\sqrt{n}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{\sin(2n)}{1 + \sqrt{n}}
$

We want to determine whether it converges or diverges, and if it converges, find the limit.


 Step 1: Analyze the numerator and denominator

- $\sin(2n)$ oscillates between $-1$ and $1$ as $n \to \infty$.

- $1 + \sqrt{n} \to \infty$

So we have a bounded numerator and an unbounded denominator. This suggests that the fraction might tend to 0.



 Step 2: Use inequalities and the Squeeze Theorem

We know:

$
-1 \leq \sin(2n) \leq 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{-1}{1 + \sqrt{n}} \leq a_n \leq \frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{n}}
$

As $n \to \infty$, both bounds tend to 0. Therefore, by the Squeeze Theorem:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

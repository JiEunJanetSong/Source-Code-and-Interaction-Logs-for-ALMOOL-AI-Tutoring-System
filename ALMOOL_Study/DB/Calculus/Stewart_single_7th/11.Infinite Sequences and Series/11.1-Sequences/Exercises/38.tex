Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$\left\{ \frac{\ln n}{\ln 2\ n} \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{\ln n}{\ln 2 \cdot n}.
$



 1. Rewrite and Simplify

We can factor out the constant $\frac{1}{\ln 2}$:

$
a_n = \frac{1}{\ln 2} \cdot \frac{\ln n}{n}.
$



 2. Analyze the Limit

It is a well-known limit that

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\ln n}{n} = 0,
$

since $\ln n$ grows much more slowly than $n$.

Hence,

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n
= \frac{1}{\ln 2} \,\cdot\, \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\ln n}{n}
= \frac{1}{\ln 2} \cdot 0
= 0.
$



 3. Conclusion

- The sequence $\displaystyle \left\{ \frac{\ln n}{\ln 2 \cdot n} \right\}$ converges.

- Its limit is

$
\boxed{0.}
$

Find a formula for the general term $a_n$ of the sequence, assuming that the pattern of the Ô¨Årst few terms continues.

$$\left\{ 1, -\frac{1}{3}, \frac{1}{9}, -\frac{1}{27}, \frac{1}{81}, \cdots \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\begin{gathered}
a_1=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{1-1}=1 \\
a_2=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{2-1}=-\frac{1}{3} \\
a_3=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{3-1}=\frac{1}{9} \\
a_4=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{4-1}=-\frac{1}{27} \\
a_5=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{5-1}=\frac{1}{81} \\
a_n=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}
\end{gathered}\\
&a_n=\left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}
\end{aligned}
$$

Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=2^{-n}\cos {n\pi}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = 2^{-n} \cos(n\pi)
$



 Step 1: Understand the behavior of $\cos(n\pi)$

Recall that:

$
\cos(n\pi) = (-1)^n
$

So the sequence becomes:

$
a_n = 2^{-n} \cdot (-1)^n = \frac{(-1)^n}{2^n}
$

This is an alternating sequence whose absolute value is:

$
|a_n| = \frac{1}{2^n}
\to 0 \quad \text{as } n \to \infty
$

Because $\frac{1}{2^n} \to 0$, and the factor $(-1)^n$ just alternates the sign.



 Step 2: Take the limit

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{2^n} = 0
$

The sign changes, but the magnitude tends to 0.

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

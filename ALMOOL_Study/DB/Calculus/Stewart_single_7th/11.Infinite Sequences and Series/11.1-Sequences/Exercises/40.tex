Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{\tan^{-1}n}{n}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{\tan^{-1} n}{n}
$

We want to determine whether it converges or diverges, and if it converges, find the limit as $n \to \infty$.



 Step 1: Analyze the behavior of the numerator and denominator

- $\tan^{-1} n$ is the inverse tangent function.

- As $n \to \infty$, we know that:

$
\tan^{-1} n \to \frac{\pi}{2}
$

- The denominator $n \to \infty$

So the sequence becomes:

$
a_n = \frac{\tan^{-1} n}{n} \to \frac{\pi/2}{\infty} = 0
$



 Step 2: Take the limit

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\tan^{-1} n}{n} = 0
$

Since the numerator approaches a finite value and the denominator grows without bound, the whole fraction approaches 0.

 Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{3+5n^2}{n+n^2}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{3 + 5n^2}{n + n^2}
$

 Step 1: Identify Dominant Terms

As $n \to \infty$, the highest powers of $n$ dominate the behavior of the function.

- Numerator: $3 + 5n^2 \approx 5n^2$

- Denominator: $n + n^2 = n(1 + n) \approx n^2$

So for large $n$:

$
a_n \approx \frac{5n^2}{n^2} = 5
$

 Step 2: Divide by $n^2$ to Find the Exact Limit

$
a_n = \frac{3 + 5n^2}{n + n^2} = \frac{n^2\left(\frac{3}{n^2} + 5\right)}{n^2\left(\frac{1}{n} + 1\right)} = \frac{\frac{3}{n^2} + 5}{\frac{1}{n} + 1}
$

Now take the limit as $n \to \infty$:

- $\frac{3}{n^2} \to 0$

- $\frac{1}{n} \to 0$

So:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \frac{0 + 5}{0 + 1} = 5
$

 Final Answer:

- The sequence converges.

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{5}
$

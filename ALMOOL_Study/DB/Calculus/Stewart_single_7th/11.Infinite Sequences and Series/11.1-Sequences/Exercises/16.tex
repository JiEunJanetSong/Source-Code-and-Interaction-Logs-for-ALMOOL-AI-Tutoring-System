Find a formula for the general term $a_n$ of the sequence, assuming that the pattern of the Ô¨Årst few terms continues.

$$\left\{ 5, 8, 11, 14, 17, \cdots \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Note that:

$$
\begin{gathered}
a_2-a_1=8-5=3 \\
a_3-a_2=11-8=3 \\
a_4-a_3=14-11=3 \\
a_5-a_4=17-14=3
\end{gathered}
$$


Therefore, we can write

$$
a_n-a_{n-1}=3
$$


Consider the following sum

$$
\begin{gathered}
\underbrace{\left[a_n-a_{n-1}\right]+\left[a_{n-1}-a_{n-2}\right]+\cdots+\left[a_3-a_2\right]+\left[a_2-a_1\right]}_{(n-1) \text { terms }}=(n-1) \cdot 3 \\
a_n-a_1=3 n-3
\end{gathered}
$$


Substitute $a_1=5$

$$
a_n-5=3 n-3
$$


Add 5 to both sides

$$
a_n=3 n+2
$$

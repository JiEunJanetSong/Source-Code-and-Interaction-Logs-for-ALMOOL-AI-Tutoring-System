Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{n^3}{n^3+1}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{n^3}{n^3 + 1}
$

Step 1: Analyze the Behavior as $n \to \infty$

Both the numerator and denominator are degree 3 polynomials in $n$, so the dominant term in both is $n^3$.

We can divide both numerator and denominator by $n^3$ to simplify:

$
a_n = \frac{n^3}{n^3 + 1} = \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{n^3}}
$

 Step 2: Take the Limit

As $n \to \infty$, $\frac{1}{n^3} \to 0$, so:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \frac{1}{1 + 0} = 1
$

 Final Answer:

- The sequence converges.

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{1}
$

Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=1-(0.2)^n$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = 1 - (0.2)^n
$

 Step 1: Understand the Behavior of the Sequence

- The term $(0.2)^n$ is a positive number less than 1 raised to the power $n$.

- Since $0 < 0.2 < 1$, we know:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} (0.2)^n = 0
$

 Step 2: Apply the Limit

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left( 1 - (0.2)^n \right) = 1 - 0 = 1
$

 Step 3: Conclusion

- The sequence converges.

- Its limit is:

$
\boxed{1}
$

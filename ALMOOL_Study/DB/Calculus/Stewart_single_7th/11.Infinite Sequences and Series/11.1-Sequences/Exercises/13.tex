Find a formula for the general term $a_n$ of the sequence, assuming that the pattern of the Ô¨Årst few terms continues.

$$\left\{ 1, \frac{1}{3}, \frac{1}{5}, \frac{1}{7}, \frac{1}{9}, \cdots \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Observe the Pattern
The numerators are all 1, and the denominators are increasing odd numbers:
$
1, 3, 5, 7, 9, \dots
$

These can be expressed as:
$
2n - 1 \quad \text{for } n = 1, 2, 3, 4, \dots
$

 Step 2: General Term
Each term of the sequence is:

$
a_n = \frac{1}{2n - 1}
$

  Final Answer:
$
\boxed{a_n = \frac{1}{2n - 1}}
$

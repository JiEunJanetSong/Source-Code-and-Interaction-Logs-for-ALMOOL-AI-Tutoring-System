Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{n^2}{\sqrt{n^3+4n}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{n^2}{\sqrt{n^3 + 4n}}
$

Step 1: Analyze Growth of Numerator and Denominator

- Numerator: $n^2$

- Denominator: $\sqrt{n^3 + 4n}$

As $n \to \infty$, the dominant term inside the square root is $n^3$, so:

$
\sqrt{n^3 + 4n} \sim \sqrt{n^3} = n^{3/2}
$

So asymptotically:

$
a_n \sim \frac{n^2}{n^{3/2}} = n^{2 - 3/2} = n^{1/2}
$

Step 2: Behavior as $n \to \infty$

Since $n^{1/2} \to \infty$, we conclude:

$
a_n \to \infty \quad \text{as } n \to \infty
$

Final Answer:

- The sequence diverges

- It diverges to infinity:

$
\boxed{\text{The sequence diverges to } \infty}
$

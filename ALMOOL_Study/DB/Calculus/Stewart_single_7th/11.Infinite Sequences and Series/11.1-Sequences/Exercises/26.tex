Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{n^3}{n+1}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{n^3}{n + 1}
$

 Step 1: Analyze Growth of Numerator and Denominator

- Numerator: $n^3$

- Denominator: $n + 1$

As $n \to \infty$:

- The numerator grows like $n^3$

- The denominator grows like $n$

So the whole expression grows like:

$
a_n \approx \frac{n^3}{n} = n^2 \to \infty
$

 Step 2: Confirm by Simplifying

Divide numerator and denominator by $n$:

$
a_n = \frac{n^3}{n + 1} = \frac{n^2}{1 + \frac{1}{n}} \to \infty \text{ as } n \to \infty
$

Final Answer:

- The sequence diverges.

- It grows without bound:

$
\boxed{\text{The sequence diverges to } \infty}
$

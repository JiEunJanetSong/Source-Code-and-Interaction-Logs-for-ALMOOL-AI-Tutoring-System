Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\arctan\left( \ln\ n \right)$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \arctan(\ln n)
$

We want to determine whether the sequence converges or diverges, and if it converges, find the limit as $n \to \infty$.



 Step 1: Analyze the behavior of $\ln n$

As $n \to \infty$, we know:

$
\ln n \to \infty
$

So we are evaluating:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \arctan(\ln n)
$


 Step 2: Use known behavior of arctangent

We know:

$
\lim_{x \to \infty} \arctan(x) = \frac{\pi}{2}
$

Since $\ln n \to \infty$, it follows that:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \arctan(\ln n) = \frac{\pi}{2}
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{\frac{\pi}{2}}
$

Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\ln(n+1)-\ln n $$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \ln(n+1) - \ln n
$



 Step 1: Use log properties

Recall the logarithmic identity:

$
\ln(n+1) - \ln n = \ln\left(\frac{n+1}{n}\right)
$

So the sequence becomes:

$
a_n = \ln\left(1 + \frac{1}{n}\right)
$



 Step 2: Take the limit as $n \to \infty$

As $n \to \infty$, $\frac{1}{n} \to 0$, so:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \ln\left(1 + \frac{1}{n}\right) = \ln(1) = 0
$

 Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

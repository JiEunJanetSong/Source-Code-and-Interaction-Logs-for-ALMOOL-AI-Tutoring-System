Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\frac{\left( \ln\ n \right) ^2}{n}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{(\ln n)^2}{n}
$

We want to determine whether it converges or diverges, and if it converges, find the limit as $n \to \infty$.



 Step 1: Analyze growth of numerator and denominator

- $\ln n \to \infty$, so $(\ln n)^2 \to \infty$, but slowly

- $n \to \infty$ much faster

This is a classic case of a logarithmic (slow) growth over polynomial (faster) growth.



 Step 2: Apply known limit result

There is a well-known fact:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{(\ln n)^k}{n} = 0 \quad \text{for any constant } k > 0
$

In our case, $k = 2$, so:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{(\ln n)^2}{n} = 0
$

Final Answer:

The sequence converges, and its limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=\tan\left( {\frac{2n\pi}{1+8n}} \right)$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \tan\left( \frac{2n\pi}{1 + 8n} \right)
$

 Step 1: Analyze the Limit of the Argument

We analyze the argument of the tangent function:

$
\theta_n = \frac{2n\pi}{1 + 8n}
$

Divide numerator and denominator by $n$:

$
\theta_n = \frac{2\pi}{\frac{1}{n} + 8} \to \frac{2\pi}{8} = \frac{\pi}{4} \quad \text{as } n \to \infty
$

 Step 2: Use Continuity of $\tan(x)$

Since $\theta_n \to \frac{\pi}{4}$ and $\tan(x)$ is continuous at $\frac{\pi}{4}$, we can take the limit inside:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \tan\left( \lim_{n \to \infty} \theta_n \right) = \tan\left( \frac{\pi}{4} \right) = 1
$

Final Answer:

- The sequence converges

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{1}
$

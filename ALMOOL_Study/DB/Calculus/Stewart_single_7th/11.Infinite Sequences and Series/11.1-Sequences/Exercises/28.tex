Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n={\frac{3^{n+2}}{5^n}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = \frac{3^{n+2}}{5^n}
$

 Step 1: Simplify the Expression

We can simplify the numerator:

$
3^{n+2} = 3^n \cdot 3^2 = 9 \cdot 3^n
$

So the sequence becomes:

$
a_n = \frac{9 \cdot 3^n}{5^n} = 9 \cdot \left( \frac{3}{5} \right)^n
$

 Step 2: Analyze the Behavior as $n \to \infty$

- Since $\frac{3}{5} < 1$, we know:

$
\left( \frac{3}{5} \right)^n \to 0 \text{ as } n \to \infty
$

So:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 9 \cdot 0 = 0
$

 Final Answer:

- The sequence converges

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{0}
$

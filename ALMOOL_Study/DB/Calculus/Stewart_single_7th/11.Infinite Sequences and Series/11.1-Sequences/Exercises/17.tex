Find a formula for the general term $a_n$ of the sequence, assuming that the pattern of the Ô¨Årst few terms continues.

$$\left\{ \frac{1}{2}, -\frac{4}{3}, \frac{9}{4}, -\frac{16}{5}, \frac{25}{6}, \cdots \right\}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& a_n=b_n \times \frac{c_n}{d_n} \\
& b_n=(-1)^{n+1} \\
& c_n=n^2 \\
& d_n=n+1 \\
& a_n=(-1)^{n+1} \times \frac{n^2}{n+1}
\end{aligned}
$$


Step 2

$$
\begin{aligned}
& a_1=(-1)^{1+1} \times \frac{1^2}{1+1}=\frac{1}{2} \\
& a_2=(-1)^{2+1} \times \frac{2^2}{2+1}=-\frac{4}{3} \\
& a_3=(-1)^{3+1} \times \frac{3^2}{3+1}=\frac{9}{4} \\
& a_4=(-1)^{4+1} \times \frac{4^2}{4+1}=-\frac{16}{5} \\
& a_5=(-1)^{5+1} \times \frac{5^2}{5+1}=\frac{25}{6}
\end{aligned}
$$


Answer:

$$
a_n=(-1)^{n+1} \times \frac{n^2}{n+1}
$$

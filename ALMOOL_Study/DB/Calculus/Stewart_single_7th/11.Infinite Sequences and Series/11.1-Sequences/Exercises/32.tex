Determine whether the sequence converges or diverges. If it converges, find the limit.

$$a_n=e^{\tfrac{2n}{(n+2)}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$
a_n = e^{\frac{2n}{n + 2}}
$

Step 1: Analyze the Exponent

Letâ€™s simplify the exponent:

$
\frac{2n}{n + 2}
$

Divide numerator and denominator by $n$:

$
\frac{2n}{n + 2} = \frac{2}{1 + \frac{2}{n}} \to \frac{2}{1 + 0} = 2 \quad \text{as } n \to \infty
$

Step 2: Apply the Limit

Since the exponent approaches 2, and the exponential function is continuous:

$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} e^{\frac{2n}{n + 2}} = e^2
$

Final Answer:

- The sequence converges

- The limit is:

$
\boxed{e^2}
$

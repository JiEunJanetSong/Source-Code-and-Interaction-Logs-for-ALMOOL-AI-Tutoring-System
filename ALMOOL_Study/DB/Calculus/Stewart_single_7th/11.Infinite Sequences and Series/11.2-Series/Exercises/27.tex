Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\dfrac{1}{3}+\dfrac{1}{6}+\dfrac{1}{9}+\dfrac{1}{12}+\dfrac{1}{15}+\cdots$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Identify the general term

The denominators are multiples of 3:

$$
3,6,9,12,15, \cdots=3 n
$$


So the general term is:

$$
a_n=\dfrac{1}{3 n}
$$


Thus, the series is:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3 n}
$$


Step 2: Factor out the constant

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3 n}=\dfrac{1}{3} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{n}
$$


The series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{n}$ is the harmonic series, which is known to diverge.

Final Answer:

- The series is divergent, because it is a constant multiple of the divergent harmonic series.

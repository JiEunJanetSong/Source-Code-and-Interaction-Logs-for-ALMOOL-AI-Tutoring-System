Determine whether the geometric series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$10-2+0.4-0.08+\cdots$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Identify the first term $a$ and common ratio $r$

- First term: $a=10$

To find the common ratio $r$, divide the second term by the first:

$$
r=\dfrac{-2}{10}=-0.2
$$


Check the next ratios for consistency:

$$
\dfrac{0.4}{-2}=-0.2, \quad \dfrac{-0.08}{0.4}=-0.2
$$

So it is indeed a geometric series with:

- $a=10$

- $r=-0.2$

Step 2: Convergence Test

A geometric series $\sum a r^{n-1}$ converges if:

$$
|r|<1
$$


Here, $|r|=0.2<1 \rightarrow \nabla$ The series converges

Step 3: Use geometric series sum formula

$$
\begin{gathered}
S=\dfrac{a}{1-r} \\
S=\dfrac{10}{1-(-0.2)}=\dfrac{10}{1+0.2}=\dfrac{10}{1.2}=\dfrac{100}{12}=\dfrac{25}{3}
\end{gathered}
$$


Final Answer:

- The series is convergent.

- The sum is $\dfrac{25}{3}$.

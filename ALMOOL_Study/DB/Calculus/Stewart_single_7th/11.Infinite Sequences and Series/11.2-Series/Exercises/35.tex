Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \ln \left(\dfrac{n^2+1}{2 n^2+1}\right)$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Understand the behavior of the general term

We look at the expression inside the logarithm:

$$
\dfrac{n^2+1}{2 n^2+1}
$$


As $n \rightarrow \infty$, the dominant terms in numerator and denominator are $n^2$, so:

$$
\dfrac{n^2+1}{2 n^2+1} \approx \dfrac{n^2}{2 n^2}=\dfrac{1}{2}
$$


Thus,

$$
\ln \left(\dfrac{n^2+1}{2 n^2+1}\right) \rightarrow \ln \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)=-\ln (2) \quad \text { as } n \rightarrow \infty
$$


So the general term $\ln \left(\dfrac{n^2+1}{2 n^2+1}\right) \rightarrow-\ln (2) \neq 0$

Step 2: Apply the Divergence Test

If the terms of a series do not approach 0 , then the series diverges.

Since:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \ln \left(\dfrac{n^2+1}{2 n^2+1}\right)=-\ln (2) \neq 0
$$

the series diverges.

Final Answer: The series diverges.

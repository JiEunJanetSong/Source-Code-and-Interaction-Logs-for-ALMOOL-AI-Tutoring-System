Calculate the first eight terms of the sequence of partial sums correct to four decimal places. Does it appear that the series is convergent or divergent?

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n!}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the infinite alternating factorial series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n!}
$$


Let:

- $a_n=\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n!}$

- $s_n=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k$ : the sequence of partial sums

We will compute the first eight partial sums, rounding to four decimal places, and determine whether the series converges or diverges.

Step 1: Compute individual terms $a_n$

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline $n$ & $a_n=\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n!}$ \\
\hline 1 & +1.000000 \\
\hline 2 & -0.500000 \\
\hline 3 & +0.166667 \\
\hline 4 & -0.041667 \\
\hline 5 & +0.008333 \\
\hline 6 & -0.001389 \\
\hline 7 & +0.000198 \\
\hline 8 & -0.000025 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Step 2: Compute the partial sums $s_n=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k$

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline $n$ & $s_n$ (rounded to 4 decimals) \\
\hline 1 & 1.0000 \\
\hline 2 & 0.5000 \\
\hline 3 & 0.6667 \\
\hline 4 & 0.6250 \\
\hline 5 & 0.6333 \\
\hline 6 & 0.6319 \\
\hline 7 & 0.6321 \\
\hline 8 & 0.6321 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Step 3: Analyze convergence

- The partial sums oscillate less and less, approaching a fixed value.

- The series is alternating and the terms decrease rapidly $(1 / n$ ! decreases very fast).

- By the Alternating Series Test (Leibniz Test), the series converges.

This specific series is related to the Taylor expansion of $1-\dfrac{1}{2!}+\dfrac{1}{3!}-\cdots=1-e^{-1}$, so:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n!}=1-\dfrac{1}{e} \approx 0.6321
$$

Final Answer:

- First 8 partial sums:

$$
1.0000,0.5000,0.6667,0.6250,0.6333,0.6319,0.6321,0.6321
$$

- The series appears to be convergent, and converges to approximately 0.6321.

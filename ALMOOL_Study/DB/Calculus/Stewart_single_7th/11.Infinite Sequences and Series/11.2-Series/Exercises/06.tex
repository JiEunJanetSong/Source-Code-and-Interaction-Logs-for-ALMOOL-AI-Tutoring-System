Calculate the first eight terms of the sequence of partial sums correct to four decimal places. Does it appear that the series is convergent or divergent?

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{\ln (n+1)}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Let's compute the first 8 partial sums of the series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{\ln (n+1)}
$$


Let:

$$
a_n=\dfrac{1}{\ln (n+1)}, \quad s_n=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k
$$


We'll compute each $a_n$ and accumulate them to find $s_n$, rounding each partial sum to 4 decimal places.

Step 1: Compute the values

We'll use $\ln (n+1)$, the natural logarithm, and calculate:

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|}
\hline $n$ & $a_n=\dfrac{1}{\ln (n+1)}$ & $s_n$ (partial sum) \\
\hline 1 & $1 / \ln (2)=1.4427$ & 1.4427 \\
\hline 2 & $1 / \ln (3)=0.9102$ & 2.3529 \\
\hline 3 & $1 / \ln (4)=0.7213$ & 3.0742 \\
\hline 4 & $1 / \ln (5)=0.6213$ & 3.6955 \\
\hline 5 & $1 / \ln (6)=0.5581$ & 4.2536 \\
\hline 6 & $1 / \ln (7)=0.5139$ & 4.7675 \\
\hline 7 & $1 / \ln (8)=0.4809$ & 5.2484 \\
\hline 8 & $1 / \ln (9)=0.4551$ & 5.7035 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
(All values rounded to 4 decimal places)

Step 2: Summary

First 8 partial sums:

$$
1.4427,2.3529,3.0742,3.6955,4.2536,4.7675,5.2484,5.7035
$$

Step 3: Convergence or divergence?

We observe that:

- The values of $s_n$ keep increasing,

- There is no sign of the sequence leveling off,

- The terms $a_n=\dfrac{1}{\ln (n+1)}$ decrease very slowly.

This series is known to diverge, since the logarithm grows too slowly to ensure convergence.

Final Answer:

- First 8 partial sums:

$$
1.4427,2.3529,3.0742,3.6955,4.2536,4.7675,5.2484,5.7035
$$

- The series appears to be divergent.

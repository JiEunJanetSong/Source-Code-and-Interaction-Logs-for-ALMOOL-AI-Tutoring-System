Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right)^k$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Recognize the Type of Series

This is a geometric series of the form:

$$
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a r^k
$$


Here:

- First term $a=1$

- Common ratio $r=\dfrac{\pi}{3}$

Step 2: Analyze the Common Ratio

We know:

$$
\pi \approx 3.1416 \Rightarrow \dfrac{\pi}{3} \approx 1.047>1
$$


So the common ratio $r>1$, which means the terms are increasing and do not approach 0.

Step 3: Apply the Divergence Test

Since $|r| \geq 1$, a geometric series diverges.

Final Answer: The series diverges.

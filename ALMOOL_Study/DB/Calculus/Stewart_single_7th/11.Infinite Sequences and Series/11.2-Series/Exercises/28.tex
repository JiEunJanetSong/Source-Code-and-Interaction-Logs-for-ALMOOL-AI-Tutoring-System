Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\dfrac{1}{3}+\dfrac{2}{9}+\dfrac{1}{27}+\dfrac{2}{81}+\dfrac{1}{243}+\dfrac{2}{729}+\cdots$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Look for a pattern

Group the terms in pairs:

$$
\left(\dfrac{1}{3}+\dfrac{2}{9}\right)+\left(\dfrac{1}{27}+\dfrac{2}{81}\right)+\left(\dfrac{1}{243}+\dfrac{2}{729}\right)+\cdots
$$


Within each group:

- First term of the $n^{\text {th }}$ group: $\dfrac{1}{3^{2 n-1}}$

- Second term of the $n^{\text {th }}$ group: $\dfrac{2}{3^{2 n}}$

Step 2: General expression

We can express the full series as:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{1}{3^{2 n-1}}+\dfrac{2}{3^{2 n}}\right)
$$


Step 3: Break into two series

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3^{2 n-1}}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{2}{3^{2 n}}
$$


Let's evaluate each separately.

Part 1: $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3^{2 n-1}}$

Let's rewrite this series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3^{2 n-1}}=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{1}{3} \cdot \dfrac{1}{9^{n-1}}\right)=\dfrac{1}{3} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{1}{9}\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with:

- First term $a=\dfrac{1}{3}$

- Ratio $r=\dfrac{1}{9}$

So its sum is:

$$
\dfrac{1 / 3}{1-1 / 9}=\dfrac{1 / 3}{8 / 9}=\dfrac{1}{3} \cdot \dfrac{9}{8}=\dfrac{3}{8}
$$


Part 2: $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{2}{3^{2 n}}$

This becomes:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{2}{9^n}=2 \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{1}{9}\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with:

- First term $a=\dfrac{1}{9}$, so total is $2 a=\dfrac{2}{9}$

- Ratio $r=\dfrac{1}{9}$

So its sum is:

$$
\dfrac{2 / 9}{1-1 / 9}=\dfrac{2 / 9}{8 / 9}=\dfrac{2}{8}=\dfrac{1}{4}
$$


Step 4: Total Sum

$$
\dfrac{3}{8}+\dfrac{1}{4}=\dfrac{3}{8}+\dfrac{2}{8}=\dfrac{5}{8}
$$

Final Answer:

- The series is convergent

- The sum is $\dfrac{5}{8}$

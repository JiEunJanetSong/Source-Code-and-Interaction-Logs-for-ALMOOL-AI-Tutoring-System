Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{n-1}{3 n-1}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Analyze the general term

Let's look at the behavior of the general term as $n \rightarrow \infty$ :

$$
\dfrac{n-1}{3 n-1}
$$


Divide numerator and denominator by $n$ :

$$
\dfrac{n-1}{3 n-1}=\dfrac{1-\dfrac{1}{n}}{3-\dfrac{1}{n}} \rightarrow \dfrac{1}{3} \quad \text { as } n \rightarrow \infty
$$


Step 2: Divergence Test (Test for Divergence)

A necessary condition for convergence of $\sum a_n$ is:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n=0
$$


But in our case:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \dfrac{n-1}{3 n-1}=\dfrac{1}{3} \neq 0
$$


So the series fails the divergence test.

Final Answer:

- The series is divergent, because the general term does not tend to zero.

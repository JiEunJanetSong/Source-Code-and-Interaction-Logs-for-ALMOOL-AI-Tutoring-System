Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1+2^n}{3^n}$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Split the Series
Break the expression into two separate series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1+2^n}{3^n}=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{1}{3^n}+\dfrac{2^n}{3^n}\right)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3^n}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{2}{3}\right)^n
$$


Step 2: Recognize Geometric Series
Both parts are geometric series.
- The first is:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3^n}=\text { geometric series with } a=\dfrac{1}{3}, r=\dfrac{1}{3}
$$


Since it starts from $n=1$, this is:

$$
\dfrac{\dfrac{1}{3}}{1-\dfrac{1}{3}}=\dfrac{1 / 3}{2 / 3}=\dfrac{1}{2}
$$

- The second is:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{2}{3}\right)^n=\text { geometric series with } a=\dfrac{2}{3}, r=\dfrac{2}{3}
$$

Since it starts from $n=1$, this is:

$$
\dfrac{\dfrac{2}{3}}{1-\dfrac{2}{3}}=\dfrac{2 / 3}{1 / 3}=2
$$


Step 3: Add the Sums

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1+2^n}{3^n}=\dfrac{1}{2}+2=\dfrac{5}{2}
$$


Final Answer:

The series is convergent, and its sum is:

$$
\dfrac{5}{2}
$$

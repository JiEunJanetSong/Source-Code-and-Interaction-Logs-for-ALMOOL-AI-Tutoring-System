Determine whether the geometric series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{\pi^n}{3^{n+1}}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Rewrite the general term

We rewrite the general term to match the geometric series form:

$$
\dfrac{\pi^n}{3^{n+1}}=\dfrac{1}{3} \cdot\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right)^n
$$


So the series becomes:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3}\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with:

- First term: $a=\dfrac{1}{3}$

- Common ratio: $r=\dfrac{\pi}{3}$

Step 2: Convergence test

A geometric series $\sum a r^n$ converges if $|r|<1$

Here:

- $\pi \approx 3.1416$

- $\dfrac{\pi}{3} \approx 1.0472>1$

So:

$$
|r|=\left|\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right|>1
$$

Therefore, the series diverges

Final Answer:

- The series is divergent because $|r|>1$.

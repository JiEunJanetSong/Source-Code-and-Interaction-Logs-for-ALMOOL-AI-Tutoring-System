Let $a_n=\dfrac{2 n}{3 n+1}$.

(a) Determine whether $\left\{a_n\right\}$ is convergent.

(b) Determine whether $\mathrm{\Sigma}_{n=1}^{\infty}=a_n$ is convergent.


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the sequence:

$$
a_n=\dfrac{2 n}{3 n+1}
$$

(a) Determine whether $\left\{a_n\right\}$ is convergent

We evaluate the limit of the sequence:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \dfrac{2 n}{3 n+1}
$$


Divide numerator and denominator by $n$ :

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \dfrac{2}{3+\dfrac{1}{n}}=\dfrac{2}{3}
$$

Answer: The sequence $\left\{a_n\right\}$ converges to $\dfrac{2}{3}$.

(b) Determine whether $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$ is convergent

We analyze the series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{2 n}{3 n+1}
$$


To determine convergence, consider the behavior of $a_n$ as $n \rightarrow \infty$. Since:

$$
a_n=\dfrac{2 n}{3 n+1} \rightarrow \dfrac{2}{3} \neq 0
$$


A necessary condition for convergence of an infinite series $\sum a_n$ is:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n=0
$$


Since this condition is not met, the series must diverge.

Answer: The series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$ is divergent because the terms do not approach 0.

Final Summary:

- (a) The sequence $\left\{a_n\right\}$ converges to $\dfrac{2}{3}$.

- (b) The series $\sum a_n$ diverges.

Determine whether the geometric series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 6(0.9)^{n-1}$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Identify the first term $a$ and common ratio $r$

This is a geometric series in standard form:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a \cdot r^{n-1}
$$


From the formula:

- $a=6$ (first term)

- $r=0.9$ (common ratio)

Step 2: Convergence test

A geometric series $\sum a r^{n-1}$ converges if:

$$
|r|<1
$$


Here, $|r|=0.9<1 \rightarrow$ The series converges

Step 3: Use the geometric series sum formula

$$
S=\dfrac{a}{1-r}=\dfrac{6}{1-0.9}=\dfrac{6}{0.1}=60
$$

Final Answer:

- The series is convergent.

- The sum is 60.

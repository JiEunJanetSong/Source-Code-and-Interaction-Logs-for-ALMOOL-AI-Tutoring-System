Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{e^n}{n^2}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Analyze the general term

The general term is:

$$
a_n=\dfrac{e^n}{n^2}
$$


As $n \rightarrow \infty$:

- The numerator $e^n$ grows exponentially.

- The denominator $n^2$ grows polynomially.

Since exponential growth dominates polynomial growth, the terms $a_n$ do not approach 0 fast enough for convergence-in fact, they grow without bound.

Step 2: Apply the Divergence Test

The divergence test says:

If $\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n \neq 0$, then $\sum a_n$ diverges.

Let's test the limit:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \dfrac{e^n}{n^2}=\infty
$$


Hence, the terms do not go to zero. So, the series must diverge.

Final Answer: The series diverges.

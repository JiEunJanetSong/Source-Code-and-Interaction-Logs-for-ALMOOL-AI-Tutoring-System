Find the values of $x$ for which the series converges. Find the sum of the series for those values of $x$.

 $$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} e^{n x}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Recognize the Geometric Series Form

We can write the general term as:

$$
\left(e^x\right)^n
$$


So the series becomes:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(e^x\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with common ratio:

$$
r=e^x
$$


Step 2: Convergence Condition

A geometric series $\sum r^n$ converges if and only if:

$$
|r|<1
$$


So we need:

$$
\left|e^x\right|<1
$$


Since $e^x>0$ for all real $x$, this is equivalent to:

$$
e^x<1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x<0
$$


So the series converges when:

$$
x<0
$$

Step 3: Find the Sum

The sum of a geometric series is:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^n=\dfrac{1}{1-r}
$$


Here, $r=e^x$, so:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} e^{n x}=\dfrac{1}{1-e^x}
$$

Final Answer:

- The series converges for:

$$
x<0
$$

- For those values of $x$, the sum is:

$$
\dfrac{1}{1-e^x}
$$

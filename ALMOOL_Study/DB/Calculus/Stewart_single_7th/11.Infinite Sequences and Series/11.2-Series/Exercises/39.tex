Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \arctan n$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Examine the behavior of the terms

We look at the terms of the series:

$$
\arctan (1), \arctan (2), \arctan (3), \ldots
$$


Recall that:

- $\arctan n \rightarrow \dfrac{\pi}{2}$ as $n \rightarrow \infty$

- But it does not go to 0 , which is crucial for convergence.

Step 2: Apply the divergence test

The Divergence Test (also known as the nth-term test for divergence) says:

If $\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n \neq 0$, then $\sum a_n$ diverges.

Here, $a_n=\arctan n$, and

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \arctan n=\dfrac{\pi}{2} \neq 0
$$


Final Answer: The series diverges.

Because the terms arctan $n$ do not approach 0.

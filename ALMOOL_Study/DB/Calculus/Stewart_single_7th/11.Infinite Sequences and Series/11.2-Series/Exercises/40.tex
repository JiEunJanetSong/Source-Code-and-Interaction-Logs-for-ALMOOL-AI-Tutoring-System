Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{3}{5^n}+\dfrac{2}{n}\right)$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Split the Series

Split the series into two parts:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{3}{5^n}+\dfrac{2}{n}\right)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{3}{5^n}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{2}{n}
$$


Step 2: Analyze Each Series

1. $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{3}{5^n}$

This is a geometric series with:

- First term $a=\dfrac{3}{5}$

- Ratio $r=\dfrac{1}{5}$

Since $|r|<1$, the series converges and its sum is:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{3}{5^n}=\dfrac{3}{5} \cdot \dfrac{1}{1-\dfrac{1}{5}}=\dfrac{3}{5} \cdot \dfrac{5}{4}=\dfrac{3}{4}
$$

2. $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{2}{n}$

This is a constant multiple of the harmonic series:

$$
2 \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{n}
$$


The harmonic series is divergent, and multiplying by 2 doesn't change that.

So, this part diverges.

Step 3: Combine the Results

- One part converges: $\sum \dfrac{3}{5^n}$

- One part diverges: $\sum \dfrac{2}{n}$

Therefore, the entire series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{3}{5^n}+\dfrac{2}{n}\right)
$$

diverges, because adding any divergent series to anything results in divergence.

Final Answer: The series diverges.

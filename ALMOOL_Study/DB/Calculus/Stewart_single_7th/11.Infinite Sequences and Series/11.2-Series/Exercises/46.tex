Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent by expressing $s_n$ as a telescoping sum. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\cos \dfrac{1}{n^2}-\cos \dfrac{1}{(n+1)^2}\right)$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Recognize the telescoping structure

This series has the form:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(a_n-a_{n+1}\right)
$$

where $a_n=\cos \left(\dfrac{1}{n^2}\right)$

A series of the form $\sum\left(a_n-a_{n+1}\right)$ telescopes, meaning most of the terms cancel.

Step 2: Write out the first few terms

$$
\left(\cos \dfrac{1}{1^2}-\cos \dfrac{1}{2^2}\right)+\left(\cos \dfrac{1}{2^2}-\cos \dfrac{1}{3^2}\right)+\left(\cos \dfrac{1}{3^2}-\cos \dfrac{1}{4^2}\right)+\cdots
$$


Almost everything cancels out:

- $-\cos \dfrac{1}{2^2}+\cos \dfrac{1}{2^2}=0$

- $-\cos \dfrac{1}{3^2}+\cos \dfrac{1}{3^2}=0$

- etc.

So the partial sum $s_N$ becomes:

$$
s_N=\cos \dfrac{1}{1^2}-\cos \dfrac{1}{(N+1)^2}
$$

Step 3: Take the limit as $N \rightarrow \infty$

We use the fact that:

$$
\lim _{N \rightarrow \infty} \cos \left(\dfrac{1}{(N+1)^2}\right)=\cos (0)=1
$$


So:

$$
\lim _{N \rightarrow \infty} s_N=\cos (1)-1
$$


Wait - there's a mistake here! Let's correct it:

Actually,

$$
s_N=\cos \dfrac{1}{1^2}-\cos \dfrac{1}{(N+1)^2}=\cos (1)-\cos \left(\dfrac{1}{(N+1)^2}\right)
$$


As $N \rightarrow \infty, \dfrac{1}{(N+1)^2} \rightarrow 0$, so:

$$
\cos \left(\dfrac{1}{(N+1)^2}\right) \rightarrow 1
$$


Thus:

$$
\lim _{N \rightarrow \infty} s_N=\cos (1)-1
$$

Final Answer:

- The series converges.

- The sum is: $\cos (1)-1 \approx-0.4597$

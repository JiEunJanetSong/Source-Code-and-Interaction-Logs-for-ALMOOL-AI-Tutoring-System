Calculate the sum of the series $\Sigma_{n-1}^{-} a_n$ whose partial sums are given.

$$s_n=\dfrac{n^2-1}{4 n^2+1}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the partial sums of a series:

$$
s_n=\dfrac{n^2-1}{4 n^2+1}
$$


We are asked to find the sum of the infinite series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$, which is the limit of the sequence of partial sums:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n=\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} s_n
$$


Step 1: Take the limit

We evaluate:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \dfrac{n^2-1}{4 n^2+1}
$$


To simplify, divide numerator and denominator by $n^2$ :

$$
\dfrac{n^2-1}{4 n^2+1}=\dfrac{1-\dfrac{1}{n^2}}{4+\dfrac{1}{n^2}} \xrightarrow{n \rightarrow \infty} \dfrac{1}{4}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n=\dfrac{1}{4}
$$

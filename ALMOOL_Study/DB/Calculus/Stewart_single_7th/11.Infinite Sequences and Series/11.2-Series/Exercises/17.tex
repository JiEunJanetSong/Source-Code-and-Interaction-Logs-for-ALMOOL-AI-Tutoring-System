Determine whether the geometric series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$3-4+\dfrac{16}{3}-\dfrac{64}{9}+\cdots$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given the geometric series:

$$
3-4+\dfrac{16}{3}-\dfrac{64}{9}+\cdots
$$


Step 1: Identify the first term $a$ and common ratio $r$

The first few terms are:

- $a_1=3$

- $a_2=-4$

- $a_3=\dfrac{16}{3}$

- $a_4=-\dfrac{64}{9}$

Let's compute the common ratio $r$ using the ratio of consecutive terms: $r=\dfrac{a_2}{a_1}=\dfrac{-4}{3}=-\dfrac{4}{3} \quad$ Wait, let's check again with next terms to be sure

Also:

$$
\dfrac{a_3}{a_2}=\dfrac{\dfrac{16}{3}}{-4}=-\dfrac{4}{3}, \quad \dfrac{a_4}{a_3}=\dfrac{-\dfrac{64}{9}}{\dfrac{16}{3}}=-\dfrac{4}{3}
$$


So the common ratio is:

$$
r=-\dfrac{4}{3}, \quad a=3
$$


Step 2: Convergence test

A geometric series $\sum a r^{n-1}$ converges if and only if:

$$
|r|<1
$$


Here, $|r|=\left|\dfrac{-4}{3}\right|=\dfrac{4}{3}>1$

Final Answer:

- The series is divergent because the common ratio satisfies $|r|>1$.

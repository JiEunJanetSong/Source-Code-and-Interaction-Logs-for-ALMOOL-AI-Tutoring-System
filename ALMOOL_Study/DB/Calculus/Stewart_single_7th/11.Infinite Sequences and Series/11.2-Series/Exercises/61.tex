Find the values of $x$ for which the series converges. Find the sum of the series for those values of $x$.

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{2^n}{x^n}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Rewrite as a Geometric Series

We can rewrite this as:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{2}{x}\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with common ratio:

$$
r=\dfrac{2}{x}
$$


Step 2: Convergence Condition

A geometric series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^n$ converges if and only if:

$$
|r|<1
$$


So we need:

$$
\left|\dfrac{2}{x}\right|<1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad|x|>2
$$

Step 3: Find the Sum

The sum of a geometric series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^n$ is:

$$
\dfrac{1}{1-r}
$$


Here, $r=\dfrac{2}{x}$, so the sum is:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{2}{x}\right)^n=\dfrac{1}{1-\dfrac{2}{x}}=\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{x-2}{x}}=\dfrac{x}{x-2}
$$

Final Answer:

- The series converges when:

$$
|x|>2
$$

- For those values of $x$, the sum is:

$$
\dfrac{x}{x-2}
$$

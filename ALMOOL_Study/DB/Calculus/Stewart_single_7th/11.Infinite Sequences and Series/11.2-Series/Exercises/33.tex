Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sqrt[n]{2}$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Analyze the behavior of $a_n$

We know:

- $\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} 2^{1 / n}=1$

So the terms approach 1 as $n \rightarrow \infty$.

Step 2: Use the Test for Divergence

The Test for Divergence (also called the nth term test) says:

If $\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n \neq 0$, then the series $\sum a_n$ diverges.

Since:

$$
\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} 2^{1 / n}=1 \neq 0
$$


This implies that:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sqrt[n]{2} \text { diverges }
$$


Final Answer: The series diverges.

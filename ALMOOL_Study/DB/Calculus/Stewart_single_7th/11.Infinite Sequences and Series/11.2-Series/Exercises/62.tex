Find the values of $x$ for which the series converges. Find the sum of the series for those values of $x$.

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{\sin ^n x}{3^n}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Recognize the Geometric Series Form

We can write this as:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{\sin x}{3}\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with common ratio:

$$
r=\dfrac{\sin x}{3}
$$


Step 2: Convergence Condition

A geometric series $\sum r^n$ converges if and only if:

$$
|r|<1
$$


So we require:

$$
\left|\dfrac{\sin x}{3}\right|<1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad|\sin x|<3
$$


But we know that:

$$
|\sin x| \leq 1 \quad \text { for all real } x
$$


So the condition is always satisfied for all real values of $x$. Hence, the series converges for all real $x$.

Step 3: Find the Sum

The sum of a geometric series is:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^n=\dfrac{1}{1-r}
$$


So with $r=\dfrac{\sin x}{3}$, the sum is:

$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{\sin ^n x}{3^n}=\dfrac{1}{1-\dfrac{\sin x}{3}}=\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{3-\sin x}{3}}=\dfrac{3}{3-\sin x}
$$

Final Answer:

- The series converges for all real values of $x$ :

All real $x$

- For those values, the sum is:

$$\dfrac{3}{3-\sin x}$$

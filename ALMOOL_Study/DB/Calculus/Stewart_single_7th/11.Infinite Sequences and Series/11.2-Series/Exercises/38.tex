Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}(\cos 1)^k$$


}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Check if it's a geometric series

This is a geometric series of the form:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a r^k
$$

with $a=1$ and $r=\cos 1$.

Step 2: Evaluate $\cos 1$

Note that:

$$
\cos 1 \approx \cos (1 \text { radian }) \approx 0.5403
$$


Since $|r|=|\cos 1|<1$, the geometric series is convergent.

Step 3: Use the formula for the sum of a geometric series

For a geometric series starting from $k=1$, the sum is:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} r^k=\dfrac{r}{1-r}
$$

So we plug in $r=\cos 1$ :

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}(\cos 1)^k=\dfrac{\cos 1}{1-\cos 1}
$$


Using $\cos 1 \approx 0.5403$ :

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}(\cos 1)^k \approx \dfrac{0.5403}{1-0.5403}=\dfrac{0.5403}{0.4597} \approx 1.175
$$


Final Answer:

- The series is convergent.

- The sum is:

$$
\dfrac{\cos 1}{1-\cos 1} \approx 1.175
$$

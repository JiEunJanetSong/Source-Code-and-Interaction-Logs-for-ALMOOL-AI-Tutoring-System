Determine whether the series is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its sum.

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1+3^n}{2^n}$$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Step 1: Split the Series

Break the expression into two separate series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{1}{2^n}+\dfrac{3^n}{2^n}\right)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{2^n}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)^n
$$


Step 2: Analyze Each Series

1. First series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{2^n}
$$


This is a geometric series with:

- First term $a=\dfrac{1}{2}$

- Common ratio $r=\dfrac{1}{2}$

Sum is:

$$
\dfrac{a}{1-r}=\dfrac{\dfrac{1}{2}}{1-\dfrac{1}{2}}=1
$$

This part converges.

2. Second series:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)^n
$$


This is a geometric series with:

- Common ratio $r=\dfrac{3}{2}$

But since $r>1$, the terms grow without bound, and thus the series diverges.

Final Conclusion:

The second series diverges, so the entire series diverges.

The series diverges.

Find the derivative of the function.

$y=e^{k \tan \sqrt{x}}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

First, let us denote the inner function:

$$
f(x) = k \,\tan\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr).
$$

The given function is
$$
y = e^{f(x)}.
$$

To find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$, apply the chain rule:

\textbf{1. Differentiate $e^{f(x)}$ with respect to $f(x)$:}
   $$
   \dfrac{d}{df}\bigl(e^{f}\bigr) = e^{f}.
   $$

   \textbf{2. Differentiate $f(x)$ with respect to $x$:}

   $$
   f(x) = k \,\tan\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr).
   $$

   First set $u = \sqrt{x}$. 
   
   Then

   $$
   \dfrac{du}{dx} = \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}},
   $$

   and

   $$
   \dfrac{d}{du}\bigl(\tan(u)\bigr) = \sec^2(u).
   $$

   Therefore,
   
   $$
   \dfrac{df}{dx} 
   = k \,\sec^2\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr)\,\cdot \dfrac{d}{dx}\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr)
   = k \,\sec^2\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr)\,\cdot \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}.
   $$

Putting it all together via the chain rule:

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx}
= \dfrac{d}{df}\bigl(e^{f}\bigr)\,\cdot \dfrac{df}{dx}
= e^{f(x)} \cdot k \,\sec^2\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr)\,\dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}.
$$

Substitute back $f(x) = k \tan(\sqrt{x})$:

$$
\boxed{
\dfrac{dy}{dx}
= e^{k \tan(\sqrt{x})} \cdot \dfrac{k}{2\sqrt{x}} \,\sec^2\bigl(\sqrt{x}\bigr).
}
$$

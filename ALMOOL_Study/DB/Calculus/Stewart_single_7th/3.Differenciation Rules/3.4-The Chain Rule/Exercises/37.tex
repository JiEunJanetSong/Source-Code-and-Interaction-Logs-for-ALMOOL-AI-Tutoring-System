Find the derivative of the function.

$y=\cot ^2(\sin \theta)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

First, let $u = \sin(\theta)$. Then your function becomes

$$
y = \cot^2(u).
$$

Using the chain rule, we proceed in two steps:

\textbf{1. Differentiate $y$ with respect to $u$:}

$$
\dfrac{dy}{du} 
= 2 \cot(u) \cdot \dfrac{d}{du} \bigl(\cot u\bigr).
$$

Recall that

$$
\dfrac{d}{du}(\cot u) = -\csc^2 u.
$$

Hence,

$$
\dfrac{dy}{du}
= 2 \cot(u) \cdot \bigl(-\csc^2 u \bigr)
= -2 \cot(u)\,\csc^2(u).
$$

\textbf{2. Differentiate $u = \sin(\theta)$ with respect to $\theta$:}

$$
\dfrac{du}{d\theta} = \cos(\theta).
$$

By the chain rule,

$$
\dfrac{dy}{d\theta}
= \dfrac{dy}{du} \cdot \dfrac{du}{d\theta}
= \bigl(-2 \cot(u)\,\csc^2(u)\bigr) \cdot \cos(\theta).
$$

Since $u = \sin(\theta)$, substitute back:

$$
\boxed{
\dfrac{dy}{d\theta}
= -2\,\cot\bigl(\sin(\theta)\bigr)\,\csc^2\bigl(\sin(\theta)\bigr)\,\cos(\theta).
}
$$

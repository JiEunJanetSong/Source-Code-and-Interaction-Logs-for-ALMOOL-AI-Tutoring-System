Find the derivative of the function.

$F(x)=\left(4 x-x^2\right)^{100}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find $F'(x)$ for 

$$
F(x) = \bigl(4x - x^2\bigr)^{100},
$$

use the chain rule:

1. Let $u = 4x - x^2$. Then $F(x) = (u)^{100}$.

2. Compute $\dfrac{du}{dx}$:

   $$
   \dfrac{d}{dx}\bigl(4x - x^2\bigr) = 4 - 2x.
   $$

3. Apply the chain rule:

   $$
   F'(x) 
   = 100 \bigl(4x - x^2\bigr)^{99} \cdot \dfrac{du}{dx}
   = 100 \bigl(4x - x^2\bigr)^{99} (4 - 2x).
   $$

So,

$$
\boxed{F'(x) = 100 \bigl(4x - x^2\bigr)^{99}(4 - 2x).}
$$

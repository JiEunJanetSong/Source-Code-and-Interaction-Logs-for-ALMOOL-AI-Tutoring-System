Find the derivative of the function.

$y=\left[x+\left(x+\sin ^2 x\right)^3\right]^4$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to differentiate 
$$
y = \Bigl[x + \bigl(x + \sin^2 x\bigr)^3\Bigr]^4.
$$

\textbf{Step 1: Identify the outer and inner functions.}

1. Outer function:

If we let 

   $$
   u = x + \bigl(x + \sin^2 x\bigr)^3,
   $$

   then $y = u^4$.

2. Inner function: $u$ itself depends on $x$.

\textbf{Step 2: Differentiate the outer function $y = u^4$.}

$$
\dfrac{dy}{du} = 4u^3.
$$

\textbf{Step 3: Differentiate the inner function $u = x + (x + \sin^2 x)^3$.}

Let

$$
v = x + \sin^2 x.
$$

Then

$$
u = x + v^3.
$$

We will find $u'$ in two parts:

1. $\dfrac{d}{dx}[x] = 1.$  

2. $\dfrac{d}{dx}[v^3] = 3v^2 \cdot v'$.  

But first we need $v' = \dfrac{d}{dx}[x + \sin^2 x]$.

$$
v' 
= 1 + \dfrac{d}{dx}[\sin^2 x] 
= 1 + 2\sin(x)\cos(x)
= 1 + \sin(2x).
$$

So

$$
\dfrac{d}{dx}\bigl[v^3\bigr] 
= 3 \, v^2 \, v' 
= 3 \,\bigl(x + \sin^2 x\bigr)^2 \,\Bigl[\,1 + \sin(2x)\Bigr].
$$

Putting it together:

$$
u' = 1 + 3\,\bigl(x + \sin^2 x\bigr)^2\,\bigl[1 + \sin(2x)\bigr].
$$

\textbf{Step 4: Combine via the chain rule.}

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx}
= \dfrac{dy}{du} \cdot \dfrac{du}{dx}
= 4\,u^3 \,\cdot u'.
$$

Recall $u = x + (x + \sin^2 x)^3$. Hence,

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx}
= 4 \,\Bigl[x + (x + \sin^2 x)^3\Bigr]^3
  \;\times\;
  \Bigl[\,1 + 3\,(x + \sin^2 x)^2\,\bigl(1 + \sin(2x)\bigr)\Bigr].
$$

\textbf{Final Answer}

$$
\boxed{
\dfrac{dy}{dx}
= 4 \,\Bigl[x + \bigl(x + \sin^2 x\bigr)^3\Bigr]^3 
  \Bigl[1 + 3\,\bigl(x + \sin^2 x\bigr)^2\bigl(1 + \sin(2x)\bigr)\Bigr].
}
$$

Find the derivative of the function.

$y=10^{1-x^2}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want the derivative of

$$
y = 10^{\,1 - x^2}.
$$

A convenient approach is to rewrite $10^u$ as $e^{u\ln 10}$. Let

$$
u(x) = 1 - x^2.
$$

Then

$$
10^{u(x)} = e^{u(x)\,\ln(10)}.
$$

Denote $g(x) = u(x)\,\ln(10)$. Then

$$
y 
= e^{g(x)}
= e^{(1 - x^2)\,\ln(10)}.
$$

Step 1. Differentiate $g(x)$:

$$
g(x) = (1 - x^2)\,\ln(10).
$$
$$
g'(x) = \ln(10)\,\dfrac{d}{dx}[1 - x^2] = \ln(10)\,(0 - 2x) = -2x\,\ln(10).
$$

Step 2. Differentiate $y$ using the chain rule for $e^{g(x)}$:

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx} 
= e^{g(x)} \cdot g'(x)
= e^{(1 - x^2)\,\ln(10)}\,\bigl[-2x\,\ln(10)\bigr].
$$

Finally, rewrite $e^{(1 - x^2)\,\ln(10)}$ as $10^{\,1 - x^2}$:

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx}
= 10^{\,1 - x^2}\,\bigl(-2x\,\ln(10)\bigr)
= -2x\,\ln(10)\,10^{\,1 - x^2}.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{
y' 
= -2x\,\ln(10)\,10^{\,1 - x^2}.
}
$$

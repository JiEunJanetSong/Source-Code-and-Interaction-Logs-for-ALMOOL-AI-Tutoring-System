Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=\sin (\sin x), \quad(\pi, 0)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given:

- Function: $ y = \sin(\sin x)$

- Point: $ (\pi, 0)$

We want the **equation of the tangent line** at that point.

Step 1: Find the derivative $ y'$

Use the **chain rule**:

$$
y = \sin(\sin x) \Rightarrow y' = \cos(\sin x) \cdot \cos x
$$

Step 2: Evaluate the derivative at $ x = \pi$

We need to compute:

$$
y'(\pi) = \cos(\sin \pi) \cdot \cos \pi
$$

- $ \sin \pi = 0$

- $ \cos(0) = 1$

- $ \cos \pi = -1$

So:

$$
y'(\pi) = 1 \cdot (-1) = -1
$$

This is the **slope** of the tangent line at $ x = \pi$.


Step 3: Use point-slope form

Point: $ (\pi, 0)$, Slope: $ m = -1$

$$
y - 0 = -1(x - \pi) \Rightarrow y = -x + \pi
$$


Final Answer:

$$
\boxed{y = -x + \pi}
$$

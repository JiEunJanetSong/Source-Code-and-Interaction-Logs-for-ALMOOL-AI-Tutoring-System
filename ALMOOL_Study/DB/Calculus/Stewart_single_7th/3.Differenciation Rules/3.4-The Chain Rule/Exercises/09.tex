Find the derivative of the function.

$F(x)=\sqrt{1-2 x}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find the derivative of 

$$
F(x) = \sqrt{1-2x} = (1 - 2x)^{\tfrac12},
$$

use the chain rule:

1. Let $u = 1 - 2x$. Then $F(x) = u^{\dfrac12}$.

2. Compute $\dfrac{du}{dx} = -2$.

3. Apply the chain rule:

   $$
   F'(x) 
   = \dfrac12 (1-2x)^{-\tfrac12} \cdot (-2) 
   = -\dfrac{2}{2} (1-2x)^{-\tfrac12} 
   = -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 2x}}.
   $$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{F'(x) = -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 2x}}.}
$$

Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=\sqrt{1+x^3},\quad (2, 3)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Weâ€™re given:

- Function: $ y = \sqrt{1 + x^3}$

- Point: $ (2, 3)$

We want the equation of the tangent line at that point.

Step 1: Find the derivative $ y'$

Rewrite the function to make differentiation easier:

$$
y = (1 + x^3)^{1/2}
$$

Use the **chain rule**:

$$
y' = \dfrac{1}{2}(1 + x^3)^{-1/2} \cdot \dfrac{d}{dx}(1 + x^3) = \dfrac{1}{2}(1 + x^3)^{-1/2} \cdot 3x^2
$$

So:

$$
y' = \dfrac{3x^2}{2\sqrt{1 + x^3}}
$$

Step 2: Evaluate the derivative at $ x = 2$

Plug in:

$$
y'(2) = \dfrac{3(2)^2}{2\sqrt{1 + (2)^3}} = \dfrac{12}{2\sqrt{9}} = \dfrac{12}{2 \cdot 3} = 2
$$

So the **slope** of the tangent line at $ x = 2$ is $ 2$.

Step 3: Use point-slope form of the line

Point: $ (2, 3)$, Slope: $ m = 2$

$$
y - 3 = 2(x - 2)
$$

Simplify:

$$
y = 2x - 4 + 3 = 2x - 1
$$

Final Answer:

$$
\boxed{y = 2x - 1}
$$

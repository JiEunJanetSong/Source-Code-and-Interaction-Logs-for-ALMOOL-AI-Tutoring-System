Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=(1+2x)^{10},\quad (0, 1)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given:

- Function: $ y = (1 + 2x)^{10}$

- Point: $ (0, 1)$

We want the **equation of the tangent line** at that point.

Step 1: Find the derivative $ y'$

Use the **chain rule**:

$$
y = (1 + 2x)^{10} \Rightarrow y' = 10(1 + 2x)^9 \cdot \dfrac{d}{dx}(1 + 2x) = 10(1 + 2x)^9 \cdot 2 = 20(1 + 2x)^9
$$

Step 2: Evaluate the derivative at $ x = 0$

$$
y'(0) = 20(1 + 2 \cdot 0)^9 = 20(1)^9 = 20
$$

This is the **slope of the tangent line**.

Step 3: Use point-slope form of the line

We know:

- Point: $ (x_0, y_0) = (0, 1)$

- Slope: $ m = 20$

Point-slope form:

$$
y - y_0 = m(x - x_0) \Rightarrow y - 1 = 20(x - 0)
$$

Simplify:

$$
\boxed{y = 20x + 1}
$$


Final Answer:

$$
\boxed{y = 20x + 1}
$$

Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=\sin x+\sin ^2 x, \quad(0,0)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We are given:

- Function: $ y = \sin x + \sin^2 x$

- Point: $ (0, 0)$

We want the **equation of the tangent line** at that point.


Step 1: Find the derivative $ y'$

Differentiate each term:

$$
y = \sin x + \sin^2 x
$$

We use the **chain rule** for $ \sin^2 x$: 

Recall that $ \sin^2 x = (\sin x)^2$, so its derivative is:

$$
\dfrac{d}{dx}(\sin^2 x) = 2\sin x \cdot \cos x
$$

So:

$$
y' = \cos x + 2\sin x \cdot \cos x
$$


Step 2: Evaluate the derivative at $ x = 0$

$$
y'(0) = \cos(0) + 2\sin(0)\cos(0) = 1 + 2(0)(1) = 1
$$

So the **slope** of the tangent line is $ 1$.


Step 3: Use point-slope form

Point: $ (0, 0)$, Slope: $ 1$

$$
y - 0 = 1(x - 0) \Rightarrow y = x
$$

Final Answer:

$$
\boxed{y = x}
$$

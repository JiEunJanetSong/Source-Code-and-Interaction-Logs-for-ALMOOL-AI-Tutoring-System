Find the derivative of the function.

$f(t)=\sin \left(e^t\right)+e^{\sin t}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To differentiate 

$$
f(t) = \sin\bigl(e^t\bigr) + e^{\sin t},
$$

we handle each term separately:

1. For $\sin(e^t)$:

   $$
   \dfrac{d}{dt} \sin(e^t) = \cos(e^t) \cdot \dfrac{d}{dt}(e^t) = \cos(e^t)\, e^t.
   $$

2. For $e^{\sin t}$:

   $$
   \dfrac{d}{dt} e^{\sin t} = e^{\sin t} \cdot \dfrac{d}{dt}(\sin t) = e^{\sin t}\, \cos t.
   $$

Therefore, summing these results,

$$
\boxed{f'(t) = e^t \cos\bigl(e^t\bigr) + e^{\sin t}\cos t.}
$$

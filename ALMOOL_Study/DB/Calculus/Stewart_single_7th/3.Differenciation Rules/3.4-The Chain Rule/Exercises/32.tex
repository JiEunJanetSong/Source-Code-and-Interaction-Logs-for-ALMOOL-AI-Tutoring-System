Find the derivative of the function.

$y=\sec ^2(m \theta)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want to differentiate

$$
y(\theta) \;=\; \sec^2\bigl(m\theta\bigr).
$$

A convenient way is to treat $\sec^2(u)$ as $\bigl(\sec(u)\bigr)^2$. Recall that

$$
\dfrac{d}{du}\bigl[\sec^2(u)\bigr] = 2\sec(u)\dfrac{d}{du}\bigl[\sec(u)\bigr]
=
2\sec(u)\bigl[\sec(u)\tan(u)\bigr]
=
2\sec^2(u)\tan(u).
$$

Thus, if we set

$$
u(\theta) = m\theta,
$$

then by the chain rule:

$$
\dfrac{d}{d\theta}\Bigl[\sec^2\bigl(m\theta\bigr)\Bigr]
= 2\sec^2\bigl(m\theta\bigr)\tan\bigl(m\theta\bigr)
\times\dfrac{d}{d\theta}\bigl[m\theta\bigr].
$$

Since $\dfrac{d}{d\theta}[m\theta] = m,$ we obtain

$$
\boxed{
\dfrac{dy}{d\theta}
= 2m\sec^2\bigl(m\theta\bigr)\tan\bigl(m\theta\bigr).
}
$$

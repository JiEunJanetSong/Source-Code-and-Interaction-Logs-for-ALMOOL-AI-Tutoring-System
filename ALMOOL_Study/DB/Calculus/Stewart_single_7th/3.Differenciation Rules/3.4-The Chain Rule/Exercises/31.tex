Find the derivative of the function.

$y=\sin (\tan 2 x)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want the derivative of 

$$
y(x) = \sin\bigl(\tan(2x)\bigr).
$$

Use the chain rule twice:

1. Let $u(x) = \tan(2x)$. Then $y = \sin(u)$.

2. Differentiate the outer function $\sin(u)$ with respect to $u$:
   $$
   \dfrac{dy}{du} = \cos(u).
   $$

3. Differentiate $u(x) = \tan(2x)$ with respect to $x$:
   $$
   \dfrac{du}{dx} 
   = \sec^2(2x)\,\dfrac{d}{dx}[2x]
   = 2\,\sec^2(2x).
   $$
   
4. Combine via the chain rule:
   $$
   y'(x) 
   = \dfrac{dy}{du} \cdot \dfrac{du}{dx}
   = \cos\bigl(\tan(2x)\bigr)\,\bigl[2\,\sec^2(2x)\bigr].
   $$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{
y'(x) = 2\,\sec^2\bigl(2x\bigr)\,\cos\bigl(\tan(2x)\bigr).
}
$$

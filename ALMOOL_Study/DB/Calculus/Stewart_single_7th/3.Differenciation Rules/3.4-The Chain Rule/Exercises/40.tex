Find the derivative of the function.

$y=\sin (\sin (\sin x))$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

To find the derivative of

$$
y = \sin\bigl(\sin(\sin x)\bigr),
$$

we will apply the chain rule repeatedly.

1. Innermost function: $u = \sin x$  

   $$
   \dfrac{du}{dx} = \cos x.
   $$

2. Next layer: $v = \sin(u) = \sin(\sin x)$  

   $$
   \dfrac{dv}{du} = \cos(\sin x).
   $$

   Then by the chain rule,

   $$
   \dfrac{dv}{dx} = \dfrac{dv}{du}\cdot \dfrac{du}{dx} 
   = \cos(\sin x)\,\cos x.
   $$

3. Outermost layer: $y = \sin(v) = \sin(\sin(\sin x))$  

   $$
   \dfrac{dy}{dv} = \cos(\sin(\sin x)).
   $$

   Hence, by the chain rule again,
   
   $$
   \dfrac{dy}{dx} 
   = \dfrac{dy}{dv} \cdot \dfrac{dv}{dx}
   = \cos\bigl(\sin(\sin x)\bigr)\,\cdot \cos(\sin x)\,\cdot \cos x.
   $$

Putting it all together:

$$
\boxed{
\dfrac{dy}{dx} 
= \cos\bigl(\sin(\sin x)\bigr)\,\cos(\sin x)\,\cos x.
}
$$

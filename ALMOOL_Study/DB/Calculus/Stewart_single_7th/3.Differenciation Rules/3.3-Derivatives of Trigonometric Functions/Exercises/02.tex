Differentiate.

$f(x)=\sqrt{x} \sin x$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Let 
$
f(x) = \sqrt{x}\sin x.
$

Apply the product rule $ (uv)' = u'v + uv' $. 

Let $u = \sqrt{x}$ and $v = \sin x$. 

Then

$
u' = \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}, 
\quad 
v' = \cos x.
$

Hence the derivative is

$
f'(x) = \left(\dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right)\sin x + \sqrt{x}\cos x.
$

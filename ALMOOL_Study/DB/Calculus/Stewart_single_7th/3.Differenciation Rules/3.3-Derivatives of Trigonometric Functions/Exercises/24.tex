Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=x+\tan x, \quad(\pi, \pi)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

1. Differentiate $y = x + \tan x$:

$
y' = \dfrac{d}{dx}(x) + \dfrac{d}{dx}(\tan x) 
    = 1 + \sec^2 x.
$

2. Evaluate the derivative at $x = \pi$:

$
m = y'(\pi) 
= 1 + \sec^2(\pi).
$

Since $\cos \pi = -1$, we have $\sec \pi = -1$, so $\sec^2 \pi = 1$. Thus,
$
m = 1 + 1 = 2.
$

3. Form the equation of the tangent line in point-slope form:

The point given is $\bigl(\pi, \pi\bigr)$. Using 
$
y - y_0 = m(x - x_0),
$

we get
$
y - \pi = 2\,(x - \pi).
$

Hence,

$
y = 2x - 2\pi + \pi 
= 2x - \pi.
$

Therefore, the equation of the tangent line is:

$
\boxed{y = 2x - \pi.}
$

Find the limit.

$\lim _{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin 4 x}{\sin 6 x}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want
$
\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(4x)}{\sin(6x)}.
$

Recall that for small $x$, $\sin(kx) \approx kx$. 

Hence,

$
\dfrac{\sin(4x)}{\sin(6x)}
\approx \dfrac{4x}{6x}
= \dfrac{2}{3},
$
as $x \to 0$. 

More rigorously, we can factor out each sine as

$
\dfrac{\sin(4x)}{\sin(6x)}
= \dfrac{4x}{6x} \cdot \dfrac{\sin(4x)/(4x)}{\sin(6x)/(6x)}.
$

As $x \to 0$, both $\sin(4x)/(4x) \to 1$ and $\sin(6x)/(6x) \to 1$. Therefore,

$
\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(4x)}{\sin(6x)}
= \dfrac{4}{6} \times \dfrac{1}{1}
= \dfrac{2}{3}.
$

Hence,

$
\boxed{\dfrac{2}{3}}.
$

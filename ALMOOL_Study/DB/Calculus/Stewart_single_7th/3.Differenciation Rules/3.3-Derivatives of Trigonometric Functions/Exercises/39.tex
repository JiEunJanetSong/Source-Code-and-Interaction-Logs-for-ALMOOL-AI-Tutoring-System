Find the limit.

$\lim _{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin 3 x}{x}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Consider
$
\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(3x)}{x}.
$

Use the standard limit $\lim_{u \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(u)}{u} = 1$. 

Here, let $u = 3x$. 

Then as $x \to 0$, we also have $u \to 0$. 

Rewrite the expression:

$
\dfrac{\sin(3x)}{x}
= \dfrac{\sin(3x)}{3x} \,\cdot 3.
$

Since $\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(3x)}{3x} = 1$, it follows that

$
\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(3x)}{x} 
= 3 \times 1 
= 3.
$

Hence,

$
\boxed{3}.
$

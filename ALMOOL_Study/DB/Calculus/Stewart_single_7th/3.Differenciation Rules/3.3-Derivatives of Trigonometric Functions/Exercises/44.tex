Find the limit.

$\lim _{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin 3 x \sin 5 x}{x^2}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Using small-angle approximations or product expansions:

We want
$
\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(3x)\,\sin(5x)}{x^2}.
$

Approach 1: Direct small-angle approximation

For small $x$, $\sin(\alpha x) \approx \alpha x$. Thus,
$
\sin(3x) \approx 3x, 
\quad
\sin(5x) \approx 5x.
$

Hence,
$
\sin(3x)\,\sin(5x) \approx (3x)(5x) = 15x^2.
$

Then
$
\dfrac{\sin(3x)\,\sin(5x)}{x^2} 
\approx \dfrac{15x^2}{x^2} 
= 15,
$
and the limit is $15$.

Approach 2: Use $\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(ax)}{ax} = 1$ and factor

$
\dfrac{\sin(3x)\,\sin(5x)}{x^2}
= \Bigl(\sin(3x)\Bigr)\Bigl(\sin(5x)\Bigr) \cdot \dfrac{1}{x^2}.
$

Rewrite each sine with its own factor:

$
= \dfrac{\sin(3x)}{3x} \cdot \dfrac{\sin(5x)}{5x} \cdot (3x)(5x) \cdot \dfrac{1}{x^2}.
$

Combine:

$
= \biggl(\dfrac{\sin(3x)}{3x}\biggr)\,\biggl(\dfrac{\sin(5x)}{5x}\biggr)\,(15x^2)\,\dfrac{1}{x^2}.
$
$
= \biggl(\dfrac{\sin(3x)}{3x}\biggr)\,\biggl(\dfrac{\sin(5x)}{5x}\biggr)\,(15).
$
As $x \to 0$, $\dfrac{\sin(3x)}{3x} \to 1$ and $\dfrac{\sin(5x)}{5x} \to 1$. 

Therefore,

$
\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin(3x)\,\sin(5x)}{x^2}
= 1 \times 1 \times 15
= 15.
$

Hence, the limit is

$
\boxed{15}.
$

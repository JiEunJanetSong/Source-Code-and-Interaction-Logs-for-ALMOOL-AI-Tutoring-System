Differentiate.

$f(x)=3 x^2-2 \cos x$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Given
$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 2\cos x,
$
we take the derivative of each term separately.

1. $\dfrac{d}{dx}(3x^2) = 6x.$

2. $\dfrac{d}{dx}(-2\cos x) = -2 \cdot (-\sin x) = 2\sin x.$

Hence,
$
f'(x) = 6x + 2\sin x.
$

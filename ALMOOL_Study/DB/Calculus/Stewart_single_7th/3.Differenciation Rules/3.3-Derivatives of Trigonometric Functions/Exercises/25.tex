Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

(a) Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve $y=2 x \sin x$ at the point $(\dfrac{\pi}{2}, \pi)$.

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

1. Differentiate the given function: 

   $
   y = 2x \sin x.
   $  
   Let $u = x$ and $v = \sin x$. Then $2x \sin x = 2 \cdot (u v)$. 
   
   By the product rule,  
   $
   \dfrac{d}{dx}(u v) = u'v + uv'.
   $  

   Hence,  
   $
   \dfrac{d}{dx} \bigl(2x \sin x\bigr) = 2 \bigl[ (x)' \sin x + x \cos x \bigr]
   = 2 \bigl[ \sin x + x \cos x \bigr].
   $

2. Evaluate the derivative at $x = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$:  
   $
   y' \bigl(\tfrac{\pi}{2}\bigr) 
   = 2 \Bigl[ \sin\bigl(\tfrac{\pi}{2}\bigr) \;+\; \tfrac{\pi}{2} \cos\bigl(\tfrac{\pi}{2}\bigr) \Bigr]
   = 2 \bigl[ 1 + \tfrac{\pi}{2} \cdot 0 \bigr]
   = 2.
   $  

   So the slope of the tangent line at $x = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$ is $m = 2$.

3. Use the point-slope form of the line:  
   
The point is $\bigl(\tfrac{\pi}{2}, \pi\bigr)$. 

The point-slope form is  
   $
   y - y_0 = m(x - x_0).
   $  
   Substituting $m = 2$, $x_0 = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$, and $y_0 = \pi$:

   $
   y - \pi = 2 \bigl( x - \tfrac{\pi}{2} \bigr).
   $  
   
   Distribute $2$:  
   $
   y - \pi = 2x - \pi.
   $  
   
   Therefore,  
   $
   y = 2x.
   $

Hence, an equation of the tangent line is

$
\boxed{y = 2x.}
$

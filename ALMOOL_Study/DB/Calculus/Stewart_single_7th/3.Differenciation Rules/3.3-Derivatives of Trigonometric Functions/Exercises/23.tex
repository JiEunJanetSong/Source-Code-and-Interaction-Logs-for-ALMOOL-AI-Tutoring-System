Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=\cos x-\sin x, \quad(\pi,-1)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

1. Differentiate the given function:

$
y = \cos x - \sin x.
$

Then
$
y' = -\sin x - \cos x.
$

2. Compute the slope at $x = \pi$:

$
m = y'(\pi) 
= -\sin(\pi) \;-\; \cos(\pi) 
= -0 \;-\; (-1) 
= 1.
$

So the slope of the tangent line at $x = \pi$ is $1$.

3. Use the point-slope form for the tangent line:  

The point given is $\bigl(\pi, -1\bigr)$. In point-slope form,  
$
y - y_0 = m(x - x_0),
$

we have $m = 1$, $x_0 = \pi$, and $y_0 = -1$. Hence,  

$
y - (-1) = 1 \cdot \bigl(x - \pi\bigr).
$

That simplifies to

$
y + 1 = x - \pi,
$
$
\boxed{y = x - \pi - 1}.
$

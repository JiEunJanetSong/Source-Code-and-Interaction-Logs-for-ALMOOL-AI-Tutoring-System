Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=e^x \cos x, \quad(0,1)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

1. Differentiate: Given
$
y = e^x \cos x,
$

we apply the product rule, $\dfrac{d}{dx}(uv) = u'v + uv'$. Let $u = e^x$ and $v = \cos x$. 

Then

$
u' = e^x, 
\quad 
v' = -\sin x.
$

Thus,

$
y' = (e^x)(\cos x) + (e^x)(-\sin x) 
    = e^x(\cos x - \sin x).
$

2. Evaluate the derivative at $x = 0$:  
$
m = y'(0) = e^0 \bigl(\cos 0 - \sin 0\bigr) 
           = 1 \times (1 - 0) 
           = 1.
$

So the slope of the tangent line at $x=0$ is $1$.

3. Write the tangent line using point-slope form: 

The given point is $(0,1)$. 

The point-slope form is
$
y - y_0 = m (x - x_0).
$

Substitute $m=1$, $x_0=0$, and $y_0=1$:

$
y - 1 = 1 \cdot (x - 0)
\quad \Longrightarrow \quad
y - 1 = x.
$

Hence the tangent line is:

$
\boxed{y = x + 1.}
$

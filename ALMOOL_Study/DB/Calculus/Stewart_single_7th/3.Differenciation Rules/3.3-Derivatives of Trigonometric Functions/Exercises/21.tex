Find an equation of the tangent line to the curve at the given point.

$y=\sec x, \quad(\dfrac{\pi}{3},2)$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We have the function 
$
y = \sec x.
$

Its derivative is 
$
y' = \dfrac{d}{dx}(\sec x) = \sec x \,\tan x.
$

1. Find the slope at $x = \dfrac{\pi}{3}$:

$
m 
= y'\Bigl(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\Bigr) 
= \sec\Bigl(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\Bigr)\,\tan\Bigl(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\Bigr) 
= 2 \,\sqrt{3} 
= 2\sqrt{3}.
$

2. Form the equation of the tangent line using point-slope form:
$
y - y_0 = m(x - x_0),
$
where $\bigl(x_0, y_0\bigr) = \bigl(\dfrac{\pi}{3}, 2\bigr)$. Thus,

$
y - 2 = 2\sqrt{3}\,\Bigl(x - \dfrac{\pi}{3}\Bigr).
$

You may leave it in point-slope form or rearrange as desired. For instance, distributing gives:

$
y - 2 = 2\sqrt{3}\,x - \dfrac{2\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{3}, 
$
$
y = 2\sqrt{3}\,x - \dfrac{2\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{3} + 2.
$

Either form is correct.

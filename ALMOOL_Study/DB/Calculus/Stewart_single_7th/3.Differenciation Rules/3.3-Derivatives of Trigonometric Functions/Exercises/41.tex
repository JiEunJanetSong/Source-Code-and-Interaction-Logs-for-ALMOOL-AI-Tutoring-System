Find the limit.

$\lim _{t \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\tan 6 t}{\sin 2 t}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

We want 
$
\lim_{t \to 0} \dfrac{\tan(6t)}{\sin(2t)}.
$

For small $t$, we use the approximations $\tan(6t) \approx 6t$ and $\sin(2t) \approx 2t$. 

Therefore,

$
\dfrac{\tan(6t)}{\sin(2t)} 
\approx \dfrac{6t}{2t} 
= 3.
$

To be more rigorous, we can apply the standard limits $\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\tan x}{x} = 1$ and $\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin x}{x} = 1$. 

Specifically:

$
\dfrac{\tan(6t)}{\sin(2t)} 
= \dfrac{\tan(6t)}{6t} \cdot \dfrac{2t}{\sin(2t)} \cdot \dfrac{6}{2}.
$

As $t \to 0$:
$
\dfrac{\tan(6t)}{6t} \to 1, 
\quad
\dfrac{2t}{\sin(2t)} \to 1,
\quad
\dfrac{6}{2} = 3.
$

Hence,

$
\lim_{t \to 0} \dfrac{\tan(6t)}{\sin(2t)} 
= 1 \cdot 1 \cdot 3 
= 3.
$

Therefore, 

$
\boxed{3}.
$

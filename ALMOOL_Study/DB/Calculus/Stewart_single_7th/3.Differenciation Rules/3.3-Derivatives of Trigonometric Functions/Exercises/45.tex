Find the limit.

$\lim _{\theta \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta+\tan \theta}$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Using small-angle approximations or direct factorization:

We want to compute
$
\lim_{\theta \to 0} \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta + \tan \theta}.
$

Approach 1: Small-angle approximation
For small $\theta$,
$
\sin \theta \approx \theta, 
\quad
\tan \theta \approx \theta.
$

Hence,
$
\theta + \tan \theta \approx \theta + \theta = 2\theta.
$

Then
$
\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta + \tan \theta} \approx \dfrac{\theta}{2\theta} = \dfrac{1}{2}.
$

Thus the limit is $ \tfrac{1}{2} $.

Approach 2: More rigorous (factor out $\theta$)

Rewrite:
$
\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta + \tan \theta}
= \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta(1 + \dfrac{\tan \theta}{\theta})}.
$

As $\theta \to 0$,
$
\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta} \to 1,
\quad
\dfrac{\tan \theta}{\theta} \to 1.
$

So we can write:
$
\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta + \tan \theta}
= \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta} \cdot \dfrac{1}{1 + \dfrac{\tan \theta}{\theta}}.
$

Hence,
$
\lim_{\theta \to 0} \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta + \tan \theta}
= \left(\lim_{\theta \to 0} \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\theta}\right)
  \cdot
  \dfrac{1}{1 + \lim_{\theta \to 0} \dfrac{\tan \theta}{\theta}}
= 1 \cdot \dfrac{1}{1 + 1}
= \dfrac{1}{2}.
$

Therefore,

$
\boxed{\dfrac{1}{2}}.
$

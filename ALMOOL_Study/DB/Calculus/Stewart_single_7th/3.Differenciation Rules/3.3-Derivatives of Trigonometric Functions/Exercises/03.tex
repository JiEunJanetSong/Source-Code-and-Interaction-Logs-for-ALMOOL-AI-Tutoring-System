Differentiate.

$f(x)=\sin x+\dfrac{1}{2} \cot x$

}\SetValue{Concept}{%



}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

Given
$
f(x) = \sin x + \dfrac{1}{2}\cot x,
$

take the derivative term by term:

1. $\dfrac{d}{dx}(\sin x) = \cos x.$  

2. $\dfrac{d}{dx}\big(\tfrac{1}{2}\cot x\big) = \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot (-\csc^2 x) = -\tfrac{1}{2}\csc^2 x.$

Hence,
$
f'(x) = \cos x - \tfrac{1}{2}\csc^2 x.
$

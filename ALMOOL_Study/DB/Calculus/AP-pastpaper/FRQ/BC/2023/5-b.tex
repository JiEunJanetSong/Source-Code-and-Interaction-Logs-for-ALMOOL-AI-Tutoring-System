The graphs of the functions $f$ and $g$ are shown in the figure for $0 \leq x \leq 3$. It is known that $g(x)=\frac{12}{3+x}$ for $x \geq 0$. The twice-differentiable function $f$, which is not explicitly given, satisfies $f(3)=2$ and $\int_0^3 f(x) d x=10$.
        
(b) Evaluate the improper integral $\int_0^{\infty}(g(x))^2 d x$, or show that the integral diverges.

}\SetValue{AltText}{%



}\SetValue{Solution}{%

$\int_0^{\infty}(g(x))^2 d x=\lim _{b \rightarrow \infty} \int_0^b \frac{144}{(3+x)^2} d x$

$=\lim _{b \rightarrow \infty}\left(-\left.\frac{144}{(3+x)}\right|_0 ^b\right)$

$=\lim _{b \rightarrow \infty}\left(-\frac{144}{3+b}+\frac{144}{3}\right)=48$

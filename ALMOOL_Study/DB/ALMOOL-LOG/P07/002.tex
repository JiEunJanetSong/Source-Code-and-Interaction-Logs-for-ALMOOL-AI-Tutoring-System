To find the slope between two points, you use the slope formula:
    
    $$
    m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}
    $$
    
    where $\left(x_1, y_1\right)$ and $\left(x_2, y_2\right)$ are two points in the coordinate plane.
    If you have a lot of coordinates and you wish to find the average rate of change, or if you're identifying t he slope of a line best fitting the points (like through linear regression), the process varies slightly:

    1. Choose two distinct points from your set of points to calculate the slope using the standard formula above.

    2. For multiple points, you could plot them on a graph and use a method like the least squares regr ession line to find the line that best fits the points. This also gives the slope of that line.

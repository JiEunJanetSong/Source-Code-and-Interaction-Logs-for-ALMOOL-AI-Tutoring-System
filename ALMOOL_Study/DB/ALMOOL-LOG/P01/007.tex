    Example:

    Suppose we have a sphere with a radius of $r=5$ inches. We want to calculate the volume of this sphere. 
    
    Solution:

    1. Use the Formula: The formula for the volume of a sphere is:
    
    $$
    V=\frac{4}{3} \pi r^3
    $$
    
    2. Substitute the Radius: Plug the radius value into the formula. 
    
    Here, $r=5$ :
    
    $$
    V=\frac{4}{3} \pi(5)^3
    $$
    
    3. Calculate $5^3$ : 
    
    First, calculate $5^3$ :
    
    $$
    5^3=5 \times 5 \times 5=125
    $$
    
    4. Plug Back into the Formula:
    
    $$
    V=\frac{4}{3} \pi \times 125
    $$
    
    5. Calculate the Volume: 
    
    Multiply:
    
    $$
    V=\frac{4}{3} \times 125 \times \pi \approx 523.6 \text { cubic inches }
    $$
    
    
    Thus, the volume of the sphere with a radius of 5 inches is approximately 523.6 cubic inches.
